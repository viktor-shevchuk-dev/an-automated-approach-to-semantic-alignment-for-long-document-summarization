1. An economic obligation shall be deemed an obligation that arises between an economic entity and another party (parties) to the economic activities on the grounds provided for in this Code, by virtue of which one entity (obligated party, including a debtor) shall perform a certain action of economic or managerial economic nature to the benefit of another entity (perform work, transfer property, pay money, provide information, etc.), or refrain from certain actions, and another entity (an entitled party, including a creditor) shall be entitled to demand from a obligated party performance of its obligation.
