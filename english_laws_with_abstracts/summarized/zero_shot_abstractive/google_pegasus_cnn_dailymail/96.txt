This Law shall regulate the relations on creating, gathering, obtaining, storing, using, disseminating, protecting and securing information .<n>The core principles of information relations shall include: securing the rights to information .<n>Every person shall have the right to information . The right to information shall be protected by law .<n>The state guarantees equal rights and opportunities for accessing information to all subjects of information relations .<n>It shall be prohibited to seize and destroy printed publications, exhibits, data banks, documents from the archive, library, museum funds . Information on an individual (personal data) shall mean details or a set of details about an individual, which is or may be explicitly identified .<n>It shall not be allowed to gather, store, use and disseminate confidential information about a person without his/her consent .<n>Every person shall be ensured free access to his/her personal information, except for the cases provided for by law . Legal regime of the information on the state of the environment shall be determined by the laws of Ukraine .<n>The scientific and technical information shall be a public domain unless otherwise determined by the laws of Ukraine .<n>The legal regime of the tax information shall be determined by the Tax Code of Ukraine . Legal regime of the official state statistical information shall be determined by the Law of Ukraine “On State Statistics”, other laws and international treaties of Ukraine ratified by the Verkhovna Rada of Ukraine.<n>The state guarantees the subjects of information relations free access to the official state statistical information, except for the information restricted under the law. Prohibition of censorship and prohibition of interfering with the professional activity of journalists and media .<n>Mass information shall mean information disseminated in order to communicate it to the general public .<n>Media shall mean means intended for public dissemination of printed or audiovisual information . Deliberate prevention of legal professional activity of journalists and/or prosecution of journalists shall be punishable under the laws of Ukraine .<n>A journalist shall have the right to freely visit the premises of public authorities, open events held, and be personally received within reasonable periods of time .<n>A journalist shall have the right not to disclose the source of information enabling to determine the sources of information .<n>After presenting a document certifying his/her professional activity, a media employee shall have the right to gather information in the areas of a natural disaster, catastrophes, in the places of accidents, civil unrest, military actions and on the territories where the state of emergency has been declared or administrative and medical and sanitary measures (quarantine) have been taken, except for the cases provided for by law . Lack of accreditation may not be the ground for denying access for a journalist and media employee to open events held by a public authority .<n>Journalists and media employees must be notified in advance of the place and time of sessions, meetings, conferences, identity documents and other public events .<n>Public authorities, which have accredited the journalists and media employees, must facilitate carrying out their professional activity . Article 28. Prohibition of abuse of the right to information .<n>Article 29. Dissemination of socially necessary information .<n>No one can be brought to liability for expressing their value judgments . A person who expresses his/her opinion or evaluation in such a manner may be obliged to compensate for the caused non-pecuniary damage .<n>If an infringement of the right to freedom of information causes pecuniary or non-pecuniary damage to a person, he/she shall have the right to get compensation for it based on the court judgment .<n>Public authorities as plaintiffs in the cases on defending the honour, dignity and goodwill shall have the right to demand in court only refutation of false information about them . Article 27 shall be deleted; in Article 40: the title shall be amended to read as follows: “Article 40. Establishing the representative offices of foreign printed media”.<n>Part 1 of Article 1 shall be amended to read as follows: “scientific and technical information means information and/or data on domestic and foreign achievements in science, technology, and manufacturing earned in the course of scientific research, developmental, design and engineering, manufacturing and social activities that can be stored on physical media or in electronic form”. The Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine, within six months after the enactment of this Law, shall: bring its regulatory acts in line with this Law .<n> ministries and other central executive authorities review and cancel their regulatory acts contrary to this Law .
