Definitions For the purposes of this Law, the following definitions shall apply: aerothermal energy shall mean the energy which is saved up in the form of heat energy in the air environment; alternative energy sources shall mean renewable energy resources which include solar, wind, geothermal, hydrothermal, aero thermal energy, wave and tidal energy, hydro energy, biomass energy, landfill gas, gas from sewage treatment plants, biogas, and secondary energy resources which include blast-furnace and coke-oven gases, methane gas for coalbed degasification and conversion of waste energy potential of technological processes; alternative energy engineering shall mean the sector of energy engineering, providing the generation of electrical, thermal and mechanical energy from alternative energy sources; geothermal energy shall mean energy stored in the form of thermal energy under a solid layer of the land surface; hydrothermal energy shall mean energy stored in the form of thermal energy in surface waters; energy generated from alternative sources shall mean electrical, thermal and mechanical energy that is produced at alternative energy facilities and can be a marketable product intended for sale; alternative energy facilities shall mean energy generating and other equipment that produces energy by means of alternative energy sources, the share of which is not less than 50 per cent of the installed capacity of all energy sources involved in the facility; alternative energy sector shall mean activity related to the use of alternative energy sources for the production, supply, transportation, storage, transmission and consumption of energy produced from alternative sources; renewable energy sources shall mean renewable non-fossil energy sources, namely solar, wind, aero thermal, geothermal, hydrothermal energy, wave and tidal energy, hydro energy, biomass energy, landfill gas, gas from sewage treatment plants, biogas; wind power plant shall mean a group of wind power plants or a separate wind power plant, equipment and facilities located in a certain area, which are functionally interconnected and constitute a single complex designed to produce electricity by converting the kinetic wind energy into electricity; wind electrical installation shall mean an electrical installation that converts the kinetic wind energy into electrical energy; production of pieces for the electric power facility equipment shall mean activities related to the production, which includes the implementation of a sufficient level of production and technological operations, the results of which change the classification code of goods according to the Ukrainian Commodity Coding System (UKT ZED) at the level of any of the first four characters, and the operations carried out in the production process should not be simple assembly operations pursuant to the provisions of customs legislation; guarantee of electricity origin shall mean a document issued by an entity authorised by the Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine at the request of the electricity producer, which confirms that the share or a certain amount of electricity is produced from renewable energy sources (and only electricity generated by micro, mini and small hydroelectric power plants if hydro energy is used); “green” tariff shall mean a special tariff at which the electricity generated by electric power facilities, in particular at the commissioned construction stages of electric power plants (start-up facilities) that use alternative energy sources (and only electricity generated by micro, mini and small hydroelectric power plants if hydro energy is used) is purchased; micro-hydroelectric power plant shall mean an electric power plant that produces electricity through the use of hydro energy, the installed capacity of which does not exceed 200 kW; mini hydroelectric power plant shall mean an electric power plant that produces electricity through the use of hydro energy, the installed capacity of which is more than 200 kW, but does not exceed 1 MW; small hydroelectric power plant shall mean an electric power plant that produces electricity through the use of hydro energy, the installed capacity of which is more than 1 MW, but does not exceed 10 MW; stage of a power plant construction shall mean a group of electrical installations or a separate installation, equipment and facilities located in a certain area, which are functionally interconnected and form a single complex designed for electricity generation, which according to the construction design documentation is a part of the electric power facility. The electronic trading system provides the ability to create, place, publish and exchange information and documents in electronic form, necessary for the electronic auction; electronic platform shall mean a hardware and software complex operating on the Internet, connected to a central database and providing the auction customer, persons intending to participate in the auction, and participants with the opportunity to use electronic trading system with automatic exchange of information on the auction process in electronic form; energy cooperative shall mean a legal entity established pursuant to the Law of Ukraine “On Cooperation” or the Law of Ukraine “On Consumer Cooperation” to carry out economic activities for the production, procurement or transportation of fuel and energy resources, as well as to provide other services to meet the needs of its members or territorial community, as well as for gaining proceeds, under the law; annual quota of support for business entities producing electricity from alternative energy sources shall mean the amount of the capacity of electric power facilities producing electricity from alternative energy sources (except for blast-furnace and coke-oven gases, and only electricity generated by micro, mini and small hydroelectric power plants if hydro energy is used), which is proposed in the relevant year for distribution at auction, as a result of which business entities acquire the right to support; electronic platform operator shall mean a legal entity authorised to use the electronic platform and act under the agreement concluded with the administrator of the electronic trading system; central database shall mean a set of hardware and software in the databases and the auction module, which provide the possibility of conducting the auction in electronic form and guarantee equal access to information for auction participants in electronic form”; The terms “electricity producer”, “consumer”, “guaranteed electricity buyer (guaranteed buyer)”, “transmission system operator” shall be used in this Law in the meanings given in the Law of Ukraine “On Electricity Market”. Basic principles of state policy in the field of alternative energy sources The basic principles of state policy in the field of alternative energy sources shall be as follows: increasing production and consumption of energy produced from alternative sources, in order to efficiently consume traditional fuel and energy resources and reduce the dependence of Ukraine on their imports by restructuring the production and rational consumption of energy by increasing the share of energy produced from alternative sources; compliance with environmental safety by reducing the negative impact on the environment during the creation and operation of alternative energy facilities, as well as during the transmission, transportation, supply, storage and consumption of energy produced from alternative sources; compliance with the safety requirements for human health at alternative energy facilities at all production stages, as well as during the transmission, transportation, supply, storage and consumption of energy produced from alternative sources; scientific and technical support for the development of alternative energy, popularisation and implementation of scientific and technical achievements in this field, training of relevant specialists in higher and secondary educational facilities; compliance with the law by all subjects of relations pertaining to the production, storage, transportation, supply, transmission and consumption of energy produced from alternative sources; compliance with the conditions of rational consumption and savings of energy produced from alternative sources; attracting domestic and foreign investment and supporting entrepreneurship in the field of alternative energy sources, including through the development and implementation of national and local programmes for alternative energy development. Incentives for the production of electricity from alternative energy sources provided for in this article shall apply to: business entities producing electricity from alternative energy sources (except for blast-furnace and coke-oven gases, and only electricity generated by micro, mini and small hydroelectric power plants if hydro energy is used) at electric power facilities or their construction stages (start-up complexes), regardless from the installed capacity of such facilities, construction stages (start-up complexes), which were commissioned by 1 January 2020; business entities producing electricity from alternative energy sources (except for blast-furnace and coke-oven gases, and only electricity generated by micro, mini and small hydroelectric power plants if hydro energy is used) at electric power facilities or their construction stages (start-up complexes), which have been commissioned since 1 January 2020, and in accordance with Article 9-3 of this Law shall not be obliged to participate in auctions; business entities intending to produce electricity from alternative energy sources (except for blast-furnace and coke-oven gases, and only electricity generated by micro, mini and small hydroelectric power plants if hydro energy is used) at electric power facilities or their construction stages (start-up complexes) and in accordance with part 4 of Article 71 of the Law of Ukraine “On Electricity Market” have concluded electricity sale contracts at a “green” tariff until 31 December 2019 and put such facilities into operation within two years from the date of these contracts for the facilities producing electricity from solar energy, and within three years from the date of conclusion of these contracts for the facilities generating electricity from other types of alternative energy sources; consumers of electricity, including energy cooperatives, private households, generating installations of which produce electricity from alternative energy sources and for which a “green” tariff has been set. Specific percentages of equipment pieces shall be as follows: a) at the electric power facilities, including the commissioned power plants construction stages (start-up complexes), producing electricity from wind energy: b) at the electric power facilities, including the commissioned power plants construction stages (start-up complexes), producing electricity from solar energy: с) at the electric power facilities, including the commissioned power plants construction stages (start-up complexes), producing electricity from biomass: d) at the electric power facilities, including the commissioned power plants construction stages (start-up complexes), producing electricity from biogas: e) at the electric power facilities, including commissioned power plants construction stages (start-up complexes), producing electricity from biogas extracted from designated sites or facilities where waste storage and disposal operations are carried out: f) at the electric power facilities, including the commissioned power plants construction stages (start-up complexes), producing electricity from biogas, obtained by forced biomass gasification: g) at the electric power facilities, including the commissioned power plants construction stages (start-up complexes), producing electricity using hydro energy by micro, mini and small hydroelectric power plants: h) at the electric power facilities, including the commissioned power plants construction stages (start-up complexes), producing electricity from geothermal energy: The certificate (certificates) of origin duly issued by the Ukrainian Chamber of Commerce and Industry (its regional representative offices) shall confirm the fact that the available equipment pieces of the electric energy facility have been produced in Ukraine. The annual support quota shall be divided into shares by separate alternative energy sources: for electric power facilities or stages (start-up complexes) of electric power facilities producing electricity from solar energy, the size of such a share may not be less than 10 per cent; for electric power facilities or stages (start-up complexes) of electric power facilities producing electricity from wind energy, the size of such a share may not be less than 10 per cent; for electric power facilities or their construction stages (start-up complexes) producing electricity from other types of alternative energy sources (except for wind energy, solar energy, blast-furnace and coke-oven gases, and only electricity generated by micro, mini and small hydroelectric power plants if hydro energy is used), the size of such share may not be less than 10 per cent. The “green” tariff shall be set: a) for business entities producing electricity from solar energy and/or wind energy at electric power facilities or their construction stages (start-up complexes), by multiplying the “green” tariff by decreasing coefficients established by the National Energy and Utilities Regulatory Commission, under Article 9-1 of this Law for the following economic entities: at the level of 0.925, for business entities producing electricity from solar energy and/or wind energy at electric power facilities or their construction stages (start-up complexes), which were commissioned from 1 July 2015 until 31 December 2019 and consist of single wind turbines with the installed power of 2000 kW and more; at the level of 0.975, for business entities producing electricity from wind energy at electric power facilities or their construction stages (start-up complexes), which were commissioned from 1 January 2020; at the level of 0.85 for business entities producing electricity from solar energy at electric power facilities or their construction stages (start-up complexes) with an installed capacity of 1 MW and more, commissioned from 1 July 2015 to 31 December 2019; at the level of 0.925, for business entities producing electricity from solar energy at electric power facilities or their construction stages (start-up complexes) with an installed capacity of up to 1 MW, commissioned from 1 July 2015 to 31 December 2019; at the level of 0.975 for business entities producing electricity from solar energy at electric power facilities or their construction stages (start-up complexes) with an installed capacity of 1 MW and more, commissioned from 1 January 2020 to 31 October 2020; at the level of 0.7, for business entities producing electricity from solar energy at electric power facilities or their construction stages (start-up complexes) with an installed capacity from 1 MW to 75 MW, commissioned from 1 November 2020 to 31 March 2021; at the level of 0.975 for business entities producing electricity from solar energy at electric power facilities or their construction stages (start-up complexes) with an installed capacity of up to 1 MW, commissioned from 1 January 2020; at the level of 0.4, for business entities producing electricity from solar energy at electric power facilities or their construction stages (start-up complexes) with an installed capacity from 1 MW to 75 MW, commissioned from 1 April 2021; at the level of 0.4 for business entities producing electricity from solar energy at electric power facilities or their construction stages (start-up complexes) with an installed capacity of 75 MW and more, commissioned from 1 January 2020; b) for business entities producing electricity from alternative energy sources (and only electricity generated by micro, mini and small hydroelectric power plants if hydro energy is used) at electric power facilities or their construction stages (start-up complexes), commissioned before 30 June 2015 and whose “green” tariff is lower than or equal to the marginal (maximum) “green” tariff, by multiplying the “green” tariff established by the National Energy and Utilities Regulatory Commission, under Article 9-1 of this Law by a coefficient equal to 1; c) for business entities producing electricity from alternative energy sources (and only electricity generated by micro, mini and small hydroelectric power plants if hydro energy is used) at electric power facilities or their construction stages (start-up complexes), commissioned before 30 June 2015 and whose “green” tariff is higher than the marginal (maximum) “green” tariff, at the level of the marginal (maximum) “green” tariff.
