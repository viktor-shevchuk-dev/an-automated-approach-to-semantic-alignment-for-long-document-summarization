Article 4. Types of Charity Work 1. Public fundraising shall be considered a voluntary collection of targeted assistance in the form of funds or property among an indefinite number of persons, in particular via electronic means of communication or telecommunications, including charity telecommunications, to achieve the objectives determined by this Law. 8. A charity foundation can be created by one or more founders. The powers and procedure of the supervisory board in charity societies and charity foundations activities shall be determined by their charters. The procedure for forming and maintaining the Register of Volunteers of the anti-terrorist operation and/or implementing measures to ensure national security and defence, repulse and suppression of armed aggression of the Russian Federation in Donetsk and Luhansk regions by the Joint Forces operation shall be determined by the central executive authority in charge of shaping and implementing the state fiscal policy.
