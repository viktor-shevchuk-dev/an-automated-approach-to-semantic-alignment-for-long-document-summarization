Relationships of commercial nature include, but are not limited to, the following agreements: any trade agreements on the delivery of goods or output of services, or the exchange of goods and services; distribution contracts, trade representative; factoring; leasing; engineering; construction of industrial works; consulting; licensing agreements; investment; provision of finance; banking services; insurance; operating or concession agreements; joint ventures and other forms of industrial or business cooperation; and transportation of goods and conveyance of passengers by air, sea, rail and road; - where a provision of this Law, except Article 28, leaves the parties free to make a decision on a certain issue, such freedom includes the right of the parties to authorise a third party, including an institution, to make such a decision; - where a provision of this Law refers to the fact that the parties have agreed or that they may agree or in any other way refers to an agreement of the parties, such agreement includes any arbitration rules referred to in that agreement; - where a provision of this Law, other than paragraph 1 of Article 25 and clause 2 of Article 32, refers to a claim, it also applies to a counter-claim, and where it refers to a defence, it also applies to a defence to such counter-claim. If a challenge under any procedure agreed upon by the parties or under the procedure of clause 2 of this Article is not successful, the challenging party may request, within 30 days after receiving notification of the decision rejecting the challenge, the authority specified in clause 1 of Article 6 to decide on the challenge; such a decision shall be subject to no appeal; while such a request is pending, the arbitration tribunal, including the challenged arbitrator, may continue the arbitration proceedings and make an arbitration award. An arbitration award may be set aside by the court specified in paragraph 2 of Article 6 only if: 1) the party making the application for setting aside furnishes proof that: a party to the arbitration agreement referred to in Article 7 was under some incapacity; or the said agreement is not valid under the law to which the parties have subjected it or, failing any indication thereon, under the law of Ukraine; or it was not given proper notice of the appointment of an arbitrator or the arbitration proceedings or was otherwise unable to present its case; or the award was made regarding a dispute not contemplated by or not falling within the terms of the arbitration agreement, or contains decisions on matters beyond the scope of the arbitration agreement, however, if the decisions on matters covered by the arbitration agreement can be separated from those not covered by such agreement, only that part of the award which contains decisions on matters not covered by such agreement may be set aside; or the composition of the arbitration tribunal or the arbitration procedure did not comply with the agreement of the parties unless such agreement was in conflict with any provision of this Law from which the parties cannot derogate, or, failing such agreement, did not comply with this Law; or 2) the court finds that: the subject matter of the dispute cannot be the object of the arbitration proceedings under the law Ukraine; or the arbitration award is contrary to the public policy of Ukraine. The party relying on an arbitration award or filing a motion for its enforcement shall supply the duly certified original arbitration award or a duly certified copy thereof and the original arbitration agreement referred to in Article 7 or a duly certified copy thereof. Recognition or enforcement of an arbitration award, irrespective of the country where it was made, may be refused only: 1) at the request of the party against which it is invoked, if that party furnishes to the competent court where recognition or enforcement is sought proof that: - the party to the arbitration agreement referred to in Article 7 was under some incapacity; or the said agreement is not valid under the law to which the parties have subjected it or, failing any indication thereon, under the law of the country where the award was made; or - the party against which the award is invoked was not given proper notice of the appointment of an arbitrator or the arbitration proceedings or was otherwise unable to present its case; or - the award was made regarding a dispute not contemplated by or not falling within the terms of the arbitration agreement, or contains decisions on matters beyond the scope of the arbitration agreement, however, if the decisions on matters covered by the arbitration agreement can be separated from those not covered by such agreement, only that part of the award which contains decisions on matters covered by such agreement may be recognised and enforced; or - the composition of the arbitration tribunal or the arbitration procedure did not comply with the agreement of the parties or, in the absence of such agreement, did not comply with the law of the country where the arbitration took place; or - the award has not yet become binding on the parties or has been set aside or suspended by a court of the state in which, or under the law of which, that award was made; or 2) if the court finds that: - the subject matter of the dispute is not capable of settlement by arbitration under the law Ukraine; or - the recognition or enforcement of the award is contrary to the public policy of Ukraine. Foreign economic relationships, resulting in disputes that may be referred to the International Commercial Arbitration Court include, in particular, relations of sale and purchase (delivery) of goods, the provision of services, exchange of goods and/or services, carriage of goods or passengers, trade representative and agency, leasing, scientific and technical exchange, exchange of other results of intellectual activity, construction of industrial and other works, licensing operations, investment, financing, insurance, joint ventures and other forms of industrial and business cooperation. In particular, the Maritime Arbitration Commission shall settle disputes arising from relationships concerning: 1) affreightment of vessels, carriage of goods by sea, and carriage of goods in mixed navigation (river-sea); 2) maritime towage of vessels or other floating objects; 3) marine insurance and reinsurance; 4) sale and purchase of seagoing vessels and other floating objects, their repairs and maritime liens; 5) pilotage and ice-breaking, agency or other servicing of seagoing vessels, as well as vessels of inland navigation to the extent that the relevant operations are connected with the sailing of such vessels on sea routes; 6) use of vessels for scientific research, extraction of minerals, hydrotechnical or other works; 7) salvage of seagoing vessels or vessels of inland navigation by seagoing vessels, as well as the salvage in sea waters of vessels of inland navigation by other vessels of inland navigation; 8) raising of vessels and other property sunken in sea waters; 9) collisions between seagoing vessels, or between a seagoing vessel and a vessel of inland navigation, or between vessels of inland navigation in sea waters, as well as the infliction by a vessel of damage to port installations, navigational aids and other objects; 10) infliction of damage to fishing nets or other fishing gear, as well as the infliction of other damage in conducting maritime fishery trade.
