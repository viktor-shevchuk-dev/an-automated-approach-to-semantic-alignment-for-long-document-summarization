For the purposes of this Law, the following definitions shall apply: 1) refugee shall mean a person who is not a national of Ukraine and who owing to a well-founded fear of becoming a victim of persecution for reasons of race, religion, ethnicity, nationality (citizenship), being a member of a particular social group or political opinion, is outside the country of his/her nationality and is unable to enjoy the protection of such a country or unwilling to enjoy such protection owing to such fear, or, not having nationality (citizenship) and being outside the country of his/her previous permanent residence, is unable or unwilling to return to it owing to the specified fear; 2) child separated from the family shall mean a person under eighteen years old who arrives or has arrived at the territory of Ukraine unescorted by parents or either of them, grandfather or grandmother, adult brother or sister, custodian or guardian appointed under the legislation of the country of origin, or other adult persons who prior to arrival in Ukraine, voluntarily or owing to the practice of the country of origin have undertaken the responsibility for bringing up the child; 3) certificate on the application for protection in Ukraine shall mean a document, which confirms that a person stays on the territory of Ukraine on legal grounds for a period 1251-VII of 13 May 2014 5) prohibition of removal or forcible return shall mean prohibition of deportation (voluntary or forcible), extradition, rendition or another forcible displacement of a person outside Ukraine to whom this Law applies; 6) legal representatives of a child separated from the family shall mean the child protection services, custodians and guardians appointed under the legislation of Ukraine, stepparents, foster parents, foster carers, administration of a health care institution, education institution or another other child care centre; 7) legal representatives of a person under eighteen years old shall mean one of the parents, adoptive parents, grandfather or grandmother, adult brother or sister, custodians or guardians appointed before the arrival in Ukraine, or another adult person who before arriving in Ukraine voluntary or owing to the practice of the country has undertaken the responsibility for bringing up the child; 8) application for recognition as a refugee or as a person in need of subsidiary protection shall mean an application questionnaire of standard form, in which a foreigner or stateless person requests to be recognised as a refugee or person in need of subsidiary protection, specifying and substantiating one of grounds for such recognition specified in clauses 1 and 13 of, part 1 of this Article; 9) identification of a person shall mean measures related to the establishment 1251-VII of 13 May 2014 15) refugee certificate shall mean a passport, which identifies its holder and confirms that he/she has been recognised as a refugee in Ukraine, and is valid for exercising the rights and performing the obligations provided for by this Law and other laws of Ukraine; 16) certificate of a person in need of subsidiary protection shall mean a passport, which identifies its holder and confirms that he/she has been recognised as a person in need of subsidiary protection, and is valid for exercising the rights and performing the obligations provided for by this Law and other laws of Ukraine; Clause 16, part 1 of Article as amended by Law No. 1251-VII of 13 May 2014 22) safe third country shall mean a country where a person stayed prior to arrival in Ukraine, except for the cases of transit through the territory of such country, and could apply for recognition as a refugee or person in need of subsidiary protection, whereas such country: observes the international human rights standards in the field of asylum set by the international legal acts of universal and regional nature, including regulations on prohibition of torture, inhuman or degrading treatment or punishment; observes the international principles concerning the protection of refugees provided for by the 1951 Convention relating to the Status of Refugees and the 1967 Protocol relating to the Status of Refugees, as well as relating to persons in need of subsidiary protection; has national legislation in the field of asylum and refugees, and its respective government authorities determine the refugee status and grant asylum; will provide such a person with effective protection against removal and provides for an option to apply for asylum and enjoy it; agrees to admit a person and ensure his/her access to the procedure of determining the refugee status or granting subsidiary protection; 23) UNHCR shall mean United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees; 24) family members of a refugee or person in need of subsidiary or temporary protection: spouse; children under eighteen years old unless they are married and supported A refugee or person in need of subsidiary protection or a person granted temporary protection cannot be removed or forcibly returned to the country where their life or freedom are endangered for reasons of race, religion, ethnicity, nationality (citizenship), being a member .of a particular social group or political opinion, or other reasons recognised by international treaties or international organisations, to which Ukraine is a party, as someone who cannot be returned to their countries of origin. A refugee or a person in need of subsidiary protection or person granted temporary protection cannot be removed or forcibly returned to countries where they may be subjected to torture and other cruel, inhuman or degrading treatment or punishment, or to ones from which they may be removed or forcibly returned to countries where their life or freedom are endangered for reasons of religion, ethnicity, nationality (citizenship), being a member of a particular social group or political opinion, or for other reasons recognised by international treaties or international organisations, to which Ukraine is a party, as someone who cannot be returned to their countries of origin. If a person fails to exercise the right to appeal within five business days following the day he/she was notified in writing of the decision to deny issuance of the documents for resolving the issue of recognition as a refugee or person in need of subsidiary protection, a central executive authority implementing the state policy on refugees and persons in need of subsidiary or temporary protection shall seize the certificate on the application for protection in Ukraine from such a person and shall return the applicant’s identity documents and other documents kept with the central executive authority implementing the state policy on refugees and persons in need of subsidiary or temporary protection. The application for recognition as a refugee or person in need of subsidiary protection shall be considered by a central executive authority implementing the state policy on refugees and persons in need of subsidiary or temporary protection within two months after the date deciding to issue the documents for resolving the issue of recognition as a refugee or person in need of subsidiary protection. If there are any doubts as to the age of a child, a central executive authority implementing the state policy on refugees and persons in need of subsidiary or temporary protection, with the consent of a child or with the consent of his/her legal representative, shall refer such a child to age determination examination to be conducted following the procedure determined by a central executive authority In charge of shaping the state policy on healthcare. A central executive authority implementing the state policy on refugees and persons in need of subsidiary or temporary protection shall investigate, in co-operation with the Security Service of Ukraine, the circumstances which could prevent a person from being recognised as a refugee or a person in need of subsidiary protection under paragraphs 2–4, part 1, Article 6 of this Law. A central executive authority implementing the state policy on refugees and persons in need of subsidiary or temporary protection shall refer the person who applied for recognition as a refugee or person in need of subsidiary protection to a medical examination to be conducted following the procedure determined by a central executive authority in charge of shaping the state policy on healthcare. Following documents examination and verification of facts provided by a person who applied for recognition as a refugee or person in need of subsidiary protection, a central executive authority implementing the state policy on refugees and persons in need of subsidiary or temporary protection shall draw up a written conclusion on recognising or denying recognition of such a person as a refugee or person in need of subsidiary protection. Decisions on the application for recognition as a refugee or person in need of subsidiary protection, including in respect of minor children staying with such person on the territory of Ukraine (applicant’s family members or those under the applicant’s custody or in guardianship) included in the application of the applicant, whose recognition as refugees or persons in need of subsidiary protection is agreed by the applicant in writing in the application, shall be made by a central executive authority implementing the state policy on refugees and persons in need of subsidiary or temporary protection within one month after receiving the applicant’s personal file and written conclusion. In case of detecting any mistakes or discrepancies in the information about a person entered into a decision on recognition as a refugee or person in need of subsidiary protection, a central executive authority implementing the state policy on refugees and persons in need of subsidiary or temporary protection shall make a decision on correcting them and shall exchange documents issued under such a decision within one month after receiving the personal file of a refugee or person in need of subsidiary protection with a written conclusion. A central executive authority implementing the state policy on refugees and persons in need of subsidiary or temporary protection shall issue and provide to each person who has reached sixteen years old a refugee certificate or certificate of a person in need of subsidiary protection within 15 business days after the date the decision on recognition as a refugee or person in need of subsidiary protection is made. A child under sixteen years old separated from the family who has been recognised as a refugee or a person in need of subsidiary protection shall have the right to obtain a document for travelling abroad for a period until he/she turns sixteen years old upon the relevant request of his/her legal representatives. If a person exercises the right to appeal, central executive authority implementing the state policy on refugees and persons in need of subsidiary or temporary protection shall, until the decision on appeal is made, keep the national passport and other documents (if such are available in the applicant’s personal file). If a person fails to exercise the right to appeal within five business days following the day he/she was notified in writing of the decision to deny recognition as a refugee or person in need of subsidiary protection, a central executive authority implementing the state policy on refugees and persons in need of subsidiary or temporary protection shall seize the certificate on the application for protection in Ukraine from such a person and shall return the national passport and other documents (if such are available in the applicant’s personal file) kept with such authority. Subject to the existence of grounds specified in parts 1, 5 and 6 of this Article, authorised officers of a central executive authority implementing the state policy on refugees and persons in need of subsidiary or temporary protection shall file submission to a central executive authority implementing the state policy on refugees and persons in need of subsidiary or temporary protection on cessation or deprivation of the refugee status or subsidiary protection or on cancelling the decision on recognising a person as a refugee or person in need of subsidiary at their own initiative. Based on fully-fledged study and assessment of all the documents and materials, a central executive authority implementing the state policy on refugees and persons in need of subsidiary or temporary protection shall decide to cease or deprive a person of the refugee status or subsidiary protection, as well as to cancel the decision on recognising a person as a refugee or person in need of subsidiary protection, or on the absence of grounds for cessation or deprivation of the refugee status or subsidiary protection or cancelling the decision on recognising a person as a refugee or person in need of subsidiary protection. If a person fails to exercise the right to appeal within five business days following the day he/she was notified in writing of the decision to cease or deprive of his/her status of a refugee or person in need of subsidiary protection or cancelling the decision to recognise a person as a refugee or person in need of subsidiary protection, a central executive authority implementing the state policy on refugees and persons in need of subsidiary or temporary protection shall seize the refugee certificate or certificate of a person in need of subsidiary protection in Ukraine, and their travel documents for travelling abroad, or shall invalidate them and return the national passport and other documents (if such are available in the applicant’s personal file) kept with such authority.
