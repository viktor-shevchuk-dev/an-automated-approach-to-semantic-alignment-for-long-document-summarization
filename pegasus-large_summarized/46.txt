Definitions For the purposes of this Law, the following definitions shall apply: aerothermal energy shall mean the energy which is saved up in the form of heat energy in the air environment; alternative energy sources shall mean renewable energy resources which include solar, wind, geothermal, hydrothermal, aero thermal energy, wave and tidal energy, hydro energy, biomass energy, landfill gas, gas from sewage treatment plants, biogas, and secondary energy resources which include blast-furnace and coke-oven gases, methane gas for coalbed degasification and conversion of waste energy potential of technological processes; alternative energy engineering shall mean the sector of energy engineering, providing the generation of electrical, thermal and mechanical energy from alternative energy sources; geothermal energy shall mean energy stored in the form of thermal energy under a solid layer of the land surface; hydrothermal energy shall mean energy stored in the form of thermal energy in surface waters; energy generated from alternative sources shall mean electrical, thermal and mechanical energy that is produced at alternative energy facilities and can be a marketable product intended for sale; alternative energy facilities shall mean energy generating and other equipment that produces energy by means of alternative energy sources, the share of which is not less than 50 per cent of the installed capacity of all energy sources involved in the facility; alternative The stage of a power plant construction may comprise start-up complexes; administrator of the electronic trading system shall mean a legal entity designated by the Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine under the law to be responsible for ensuring the functioning of the electronic trading system; support quota distribution auction (auction) shall mean a method of determining business entities that acquire the right to obtain support in electricity production from alternative energy sources (except for blast-furnace and coke-oven gases, and only electricity generated by micro, mini and small hydroelectric power plants if hydro energy is used); The electronic trading system provides the ability to create, place, publish and exchange information and documents in electronic form, necessary for the electronic auction; electronic platform shall mean a hardware and software complex operating on the Internet, connected to a central database and providing the auction customer, persons intending to participate in the auction, and participants with the opportunity to use electronic trading system with automatic exchange of information on the auction process in electronic form; energy cooperative shall mean a legal entity established pursuant to the Law of Ukraine “On Cooperation” or the Law of Ukraine “On Consumer Cooperation” to carry out economic activities for the production, procurement or transportation of fuel and energy resources, as well as to provide other services to meet the needs of its members or territorial community, as well as for gaining proceeds, under the law; annual quota of support for business entities producing electricity from alternative energy sources shall mean the amount of the capacity of electric power facilities producing electricity from alternative energy sources (except for blast-furnace and coke-oven gases, and only electricity generated by micro, mini and small hydroelectric power plants if hydro energy is used), which is proposed in the relevant year for distribution at auction, as a result of which business entities acquire the right to support; electronic platform operator shall mean a legal Basic principles of state policy in the field of alternative energy sources The basic principles of state policy in the field of alternative energy sources shall be as follows: increasing production and consumption of energy produced from alternative sources, in order to efficiently consume traditional fuel and energy resources and reduce the dependence of Ukraine on their imports by restructuring the production and rational consumption of energy by increasing the share of energy produced from alternative sources; compliance with environmental safety by reducing the negative impact on the environment during the creation and operation of alternative energy facilities, as well as during the transmission, transportation, supply, storage and consumption of energy produced from alternative sources; compliance with the safety requirements for human health at alternative energy facilities at all production stages, as well as during the transmission, transportation, supply, storage and consumption of energy produced from alternative sources; scientific and technical support for the development of alternative energy, popularisation and implementation of scientific and technical achievements in this field, training of relevant specialists in higher and secondary educational facilities; compliance with the law by all subjects of relations pertaining to the production, storage, transportation, supply, transmission and consumption of energy produced from alternative sources; compliance with the conditions of rational consumption and savings of energy produced from alternative sources; attracting domestic and foreign investment and supporting entrepreneurship in the field State administration in the field of alternative energy sources shall provide for the following: development of national, sectoral and local programmes in the field of alternative energy sources, as well as their scientific, scientific-technical and financial-economic support, development and implementation of tasks of the relevant national targeted scientific and scientific-technical programmes; development and adoption by executive authorities within their competence of regulations, state norms, rules, methodological documents on the use of alternative energy sources; coordination and adjustment of sectoral and local programs in the field of alternative energy sources with national programmes; monitoring compliance with the requirements of legislation in the field of alternative energy sources and the implementation of national programmes in this area. The procedure for alternative energy sources exploitation The exploitation of alternative energy sources at alternative energy facilities shall be carried out under the following conditions: safe works, state supervision over energy consumption regimes; energy security meeting the periodic, current and future needs of energy consumers in terms of technical and economic aspects; compliance with technological requirements for the production, accumulation, transmission, supply and consumption of energy; observance of uniform state norms, rules by all subjects of relations connected with construction (creation), operation, decommissioning of objects of alternative energy, systems of dispatching (operational and technological) management; compliance with the rules of alternative energy facilities exploitation governed by regulatory acts, mandatory for all business entities. Emergency protection and environmental safety when using alternative energy sources Emergency protection and environmental safety when using alternative energy sources shall be carried out by means of: prevention of emergencies and elimination of their consequences at alternative energy facilities through compliance with requirements and rules; creation of conditions for development, increase of technical level, safe operation and protection of alternative energy facilities under the law; maintaining the necessary balance of capacity and quality of energy produced from alternative sources to ensure reliable and trouble-free operation with the unified energy system of Ukraine; supervision of the implementation of new systems of emergency automation and alternative energy facilities protection, as well as means of Participation of Ukraine in international cooperation in the field of alternative energy sources Ukraine participates in international cooperation in the field of production and consumption of energy produced from alternative sources, under the legislation of Ukraine and international treaties of Ukraine. Within six months from the day of enactment of this Law, the Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine shall: develop a financial mechanism to stimulate the development of alternative energy and submit proposals to the Verkhovna Rada of Ukraine on the legislative support of such a mechanism; prepare and submit for consideration to the Verkhovna Rada of Ukraine proposals on bringing the laws of Ukraine in line with this Law; bring its regulatory acts in line with this Law; subject to its competence, ensure adoption of the regulatory acts provided for in this Law; ensure that ministries and other central executive authorities review their regulatory acts contrary to this Law.
