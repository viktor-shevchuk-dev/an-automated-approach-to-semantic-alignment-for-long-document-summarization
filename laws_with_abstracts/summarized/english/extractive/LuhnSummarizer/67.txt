The condition for equivalence of exchange under a barter agreement shall be the exchange of goods (works, services) at prices determined by the subjects of foreign economic activity of Ukraine on a contractual basis, taking into account supply and demand as well as other factors effective in the respective markets at the time of conclusion of barter agreements. Goods imported under a barter agreement shall be subject to import into the customs territory of Ukraine within the terms specified in such agreement, but not later than 180 calendar days from the date of customs clearance (the date of execution of the customs declaration for export) of goods actually exported under the barter agreement, and in the case of export under a barter agreement of works and services – from the date of signing the act or other document confirming the performance of works, delivery of services. The date of import of goods under a barter agreement into the customs territory of Ukraine shall be considered to be the date of their customs clearance (the date of execution of the customs declaration for import), and in the case of import under a barter agreement of works or services – the date of signing an act or other document confirming the performance of works, delivery of services; 4. In the event of changes to the terms of the agreement that stipulates the replacement of obligations to pay for goods by a foreign counterparty in cash with obligations to supply goods (works, services), the Ukrainian supplier submits copies of the agreement and additional agreements to the tax authority. The breach by a subject of foreign economic activity of Ukraine of the terms of import of goods (performance of works, delivery of services) imported under a barter agreement, stipulated by part one of Article 2 of this Law, entails the recovery of default interest per each day of delay in the amount of 0.3 per cent of the value of non-received goods (works, services) imported under a barter agreement. Subjects of foreign economic activity of Ukraine who have conducted the export or import of works or services under a barter agreement shall be obliged to notify the customs authorities (if goods are imported or exported under such an agreement) or tax authorities (if works or services are imported or exported under such an agreement) of the fact of exporting goods (works, services) within five working days from the date of signing an act or other document certifying the performance of works or services. Customs authorities shall exercise control over the receipt of goods by import under barter agreements and inform the tax authorities about the breach of terms goods arrival as stipulated by part one of Article 2 of this Law.
