For the purposes of this Law, the following definitions shall apply: 1) high technologies – technologies developed on the basis of the latest scientific knowledge which, being at a higher technological level, outperform the best domestic and foreign analogues and are competitive in the world market of high-tech products; 2) technology transfer agreement – an agreement concluded in writing between holders of property rights to the technology or its components and/or the persons they are fully or partially transferred to; 3) documentation – information about the technology and its components recorded on a physical carrier (regulatory, scientific and technical, design, construction, manufacturing documentation, reports on the results of patent and market, scientific, research, design and construction works, official tests of technology components), that allow their use; 4) intangible asset – intellectual property item(s), as well as items subject to other similar rights recognised as an item of property rights following the procedure established by law; 5) know-how – technical, organisational or commercial information obtained through experience and testing of technology and its components, which: is not a matter of common knowledge or easily accessible on the day of the conclusion of the technology transfer agreement; is material, that is, important and useful for the manufacturing of products, technological process and/or provision of services; is defined, that is, described sufficiently and exhaustively, so it is possible to check its compliance with the criteria of not being a matter of common knowledge as well as its materiality; 6) technology item – scientific and applied scientific results, intellectual property items (in particular, inventions, utility models, works of scientific, technical nature, software, trade secrets), know-how, reflecting the list, terms, procedure and sequence of operations, the process of manufacturing and/or sale and storage of products, provision of services; 7) patent freedom to operate – the characteristic of technologies and/or their components which means it is possible to make free use of them in a certain state without the risk of violating the intellectual property rights to technologies and/or components of technologies in force within its territory, certified by the protection titles of this state (patents) and certificates belonging to third parties, is established by the protection titles (patents) and certificates for these items and regulatory legal acts of the state (s) where the use of these technologies and their components is planned, as well as within a specific period of time following the validity period of protection titles (patents) and certificates for technologies and/or their components within the territory of such a state(s); 8) patent and market research – a systematic scientific analysis of the properties of technologies and their components related to their legal protection, the state of technology markets, their components and products, their status as to patent and licensing requirements, the nature of manufacturing; 9) technology market – a system of interconnections between consumers of technologies and their components and/or competing suppliers of these technologies and their components, aimed at meeting public and state needs for relevant technologies, their components and products manufactured using them; 10) technology component – a part of technology that reflects individual elements of technology in the form of scientific and applied scientific results, intellectual property items, know-how; 11) technology – the result of scientific and technical activities, a set of systematised scientific knowledge, technical, organisational and other decisions on the list, term, procedure and sequence of operations, the process of manufacturing and/or sale and storage of products, provision of services; 12) dual-use technologies – technologies that, in addition to civilian purposes, can be used for the development, manufacturing or use of weapons, military or special equipment; 13) technology transfer – the transfer of technology that is formalised by entering into a bilateral or multilateral agreement between individuals and/or legal entities, which establishes, changes or terminates property rights and obligations in relation to the technology and/or its components; 14) the authorised body for the development and implementation of state policy in the field of technology transfer – the main body in the system of central executive authorities that develops and ensures the implementation of state policy in the field of science and innovation; 15) the authorised body for the implementation of state policy in the field of technology transfer – the central executive body that implements the state policy in the field of scientific, scientific and technical as well as innovative activities. The technology transfer entities are: 1) the authorised body for the development and implementation of state policy in the field of technology transfer, the authorised body for the implementation of state policy in the field of technology transfer, other central and local executive authorities, local self-government bodies, as well as the National Academy of Sciences of Ukraine and branch Academies of Sciences that participate in the purchase, transfer and/or use of technologies; 2) enterprises, scientific institutions, organisations, higher education institutions and other legal entities, regardless of the form of ownership, that develop and/or use technologies and/or their components and which hold or transfer property rights to use intellectual property items that are components of technologies; 3) individuals directly involved in the development, transfer and application of technologies and/or their components who provide the necessary information, financial and other services at all stages of commercialisation of technologies and/or their components. To perform tasks assigned to it, the authorised body for the implementation of state policy in the field of technology transfer shall: 1) support the development of infrastructure in the field of technology transfer and foster investment attraction in this area for innovative development of the national economy and technological renewal of manufacturing; 2) prepare and submit proposals for improving the functioning of state regional centres of science, innovation and informatisation, which are part of the state system of scientific, technical and economic information, as well as departments of scientific and technical, in a particular market and economic and patent information at the regional level; 3) participate in the drafting of proposals on the coordination of industry-specific training and retraining of specialists in intellectual property, technology transfer and innovation management; 4) at the suggestion of holders of rights to technologies and their components developed for budget funds, take measures to ensure the financing of foreign patenting of intellectual property items for the funds provided for such purposes in the state budget of Ukraine, as well as organise the attraction of other funds to finance foreign patenting; 5) coordinate the import of technologies to Ukraine, the purchase being provided for budget funds; 6) coordinate the transfer of technologies developed or purchased for budget funds to legal entities registered in other countries, or to foreign individuals or stateless persons and registers them; 7) keep a register of technologies developed or purchased for budget funds, records and controls the transferred technologies; 8) exercise control over the payment of remuneration to authors from the funds received as a result of technology transfer, under Article 23 of this Law in accordance with the procedure established by the Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine; 9) keep a register of technologies developed or purchased by state-owned enterprises and offered for registration by technology transfer entities (technology owners); 30) perform other functions as prescribed by the law. Central executive authorities, that manage, in particular, scientific and manufacturing enterprises, scientific institutions and organisations, higher education institutions that carry out scientific and technical activities in accordance with their provisions, the National Academy of Sciences of Ukraine and branch Academies of Sciences, that holds accountable enterprises, scientific institutions, organisations, scientific and manufacturing associations and technology parks, which can develop and/or use technologies and their components shall: 1) develop industry-specific comprehensive programs for the technological renewal of enterprises as components of programmes for the development of individual sectors of the economy and ensure their implementation; 2) contribute to the development of competitive, import-substituting technologies and their components; 3) ensure the compiling of databases on technologies and/or their components, property rights, in particular on intellectual property rights that belong to enterprises, scientific institutions, organisations and higher education institutions that are managed by the relevant central executive authority or accountable to the National Academy of Sciences of Ukraine or the relevant branch Academy of Sciences, provide access to them to persons interested in the use of certain technology and/or its components; 4) submit information to the authorised body for the implementation of state policy in the field of technology transfer about completed technologies, in particular, developed or purchased for budget funds, about technologies developed or purchased by state-owned enterprises, as well as about technologies offered for registration by technology transfer entities (technology owners), to include in the register; 5) submit information to the authorised body for the implementation of state policy in the field of technology transfer on the transferred technologies developed for budget funds by enterprises, scientific institutions, organisations and higher education institutions managed by them, in accordance with the procedure established by the Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine; 6) ensure compliance with the requirements of international agreements of Ukraine on the man-made hazard prevention, the spread of potentially dangerous technologies and environmental safety of technologies; 7) contribute to attracting investment in the implementation of industry-specific comprehensive programmes of technological renewal of enterprises; 8) prepare and submit to the authorised body for the implementation of state policy in the field of technology transfer proposals on the most effective ways to use and/or transfer technologies and their components, primarily of domestic origin; 9) submit a request to the authorised body for the implementation of state policy in the field of technology transfer on the need to provide support with patenting of technologies and/or their components outside of Ukraine; 10) participate in the drafting of technology transfer agreements; 11) ensure timely submission to the authorised body for the implementation of state policy in the field of technology transfer of reports on patent market conditions, scientific research, design, construction, testing works related to the development and use of technologies and their components, patenting and licensing of rights to them, on payment of remuneration to their authors; 12) exercise control over the transfer of property rights to technologies and their components developed for budget funds to enterprises, scientific institutions, organisations and higher education institutions that will use these technologies in their activities under the concluded agreement; 13) exercise control over the payment by enterprises, scientific institutions, organisations and higher education institutions that will use technologies and/or their components in their activities of remuneration to the authors of technologies and/or their components under the agreement concluded with them and following the procedure established by the Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine; 14) exercise control over the record-keeping of technologies and/or their components within the property of enterprises, scientific institutions, organisations and higher education institutions to which the rights to technologies and/or their components have been transferred, including intangible assets that are components of technologies; 15) perform other functions established by Law within the limits of their powers. Institutions and organisations of the state system of scientific, technical and patent information ensure: 1) development of information resources of scientific and technical, market and economic and patent information, taking into account all available sources of information about the components of technologies; 2) creation of regional branches of scientific and technical information bodies within the allowed number of employees and salary fund; 3) drafting of background reports on technologies and their components on behalf of the authorised body for the implementation of state policy in the field of technology transfer and by order of other technology transfer entities; 4) compiling of industry-specific and regional automated databases on technologies and their components developed for the budget funds, in particular on patent and market, scientific research, design, construction and testing work related to the development and use of technologies and/or their components, integration of such databases into international information networks on technology transfer issues; 5) holding of advertising and informational events, seminars, conferences, round tables, exhibitions, public discussion of information about technologies and their components in order to disseminate this information in the regions; 6) review, free of charge, by individuals and legal entities involved in the creation, use and transfer of technologies of abstract patent information about technologies and their components; 7) providing stakeholders, as per the procedure established by the authorised body for the implementation of state policy in the field of technology transfer, with a list of technologies available in Ukraine and abroad and/or their components, in particular, technological innovations, scientific and technical, including market and economic and patent information about technologies and/or their components, the state being the holder of property rights or the share of property rights to them. Technology transfer agreements shall also include terms concerning: 1) license and its scope for use of technologies and their components; 2) territorial restrictions (prohibition to use the technology transferred under the agreement, its components within the territory not provided for in the agreement); 3) restrictions on the scope of application of technologies and their components; 4) the procedure for granting sub-licences for the components of technology to third parties (if it is necessary to include third parties in the technological process of manufacturing of products), except in cases of transfer of rights to use marks for goods and services, commercial (brand) names, with the determined restrictions on the right to use technology and its components, including restrictions on the circle of persons who are allowed to use the components of technology and have access to information about them; 5) transfer of rights to know-how, feasibility studies, plans, instructions, specifications, drawings and other information materials about technologies and their components necessary for their effective use, including restrictions related to the conditions of maintaining the confidentiality of information about technologies and their components during their use; 6) carrying out works on improving technologies and their components and the procedure for providing parties with information on these improvements; 7) providing advice and services on the design, assistance and training of personnel who ensure the implementation of technologies, and management personnel of the person to whom the rights to the technology and its components are transferred; 8) insurance of technologies and their components; 9) the procedure for compensation of expenses related to the transfer of technologies, including the adaptation of technologies and their components to the conditions of the enterprise, scientific institution, organisation and higher education institution where they will be used, personnel training; 10) restrictions concerning the actions of the parties in the event of agreement expiration, its termination or the occurrence of force majeure (force majeure circumstances). It is not allowed to conclude technology transfer agreements that provide for: 1) import to Ukraine of technologies and/or their components that may harm the environment or human health; 2) making payments that significantly exceed the price of the technology and its components; 3) obligations to purchase raw materials, semi-finished products, equipment and its components from the person transferring the technology and/or its components that are not used in the manufacturing of the relevant products; 4) obligations regarding the pre-emptive sale of products manufactured using the technology to customers indicated by the person who transfers this technology and the involvement of personnel determined by it; 5) the right of the person transferring the technology to determine the sale or re-export price of products manufactured using this technology; 6) unjustified restrictions on the volume of production; 7) restrictions that contradict the legislation on the exercise of property rights to technologies and their components; 8) prohibition of the use of similar or more advanced technologies and their components; 9) prohibition or restriction of export of products manufactured using the technology; 10) obligations regarding the use of a patented intellectual property item that is not used in the process of applying technologies.
