Definition of terms and rules of their interpretation For the purposes of this law: “arbitration” means any arbitration (arbitration tribunal) whether set up specifically for a given case or administered by a permanent arbitration institution, in particular, the International Commercial Arbitration Court or the Maritime Arbitration Commission at the Ukrainian Chamber of Commerce and Industry (Annexes No. Relationships of commercial nature include, but are not limited to, the following agreements: any trade agreements on the delivery of goods or output of services, or the exchange of goods and services; distribution contracts, trade representative; factoring; leasing; engineering; construction of industrial works; consulting; licensing agreements; investment; provision of finance; banking services; insurance; operating or concession agreements; joint ventures and other forms of industrial or business cooperation; and transportation of goods and conveyance of passengers by air, sea, rail and road; - where a provision of this Law, except Article 28, leaves the parties free to make a decision on a certain issue, such freedom includes the right of the parties to authorise a third party, including an institution, to make such a decision; - where a provision of this Law refers to the fact that the parties have agreed or that they may agree or in any other way refers to an agreement of the parties, such agreement includes any arbitration rules referred to in that agreement; - where a provision of this Law, other than paragraph 1 of Article 25 and clause 2 of Article 32, refers to a claim, it also applies to a counter-claim, and where it refers to a defence, it also applies to a defence to such counter-claim. In the absence of such agreement, - in an arbitration with three arbitrators, each party shall appoint one arbitrator, and the two arbitrators thus appointed shall appoint the third arbitrator; if a party fails to appoint the arbitrator within thirty days of receipt of a request to do so from the other party, or if the two arbitrators fail to agree on the third arbitrator within thirty days of their appointment, the appointment shall be made, upon request of any party, by the court or other authority specified in clause 1 of Article 6; - in an arbitration with a sole arbitrator, if the parties fail to agree on the arbitrator, he or she shall be appointed, upon request of any party, by the authority specified in clause 1 of Article 6. If a challenge under any procedure agreed upon by the parties or under the procedure of clause 2 of this Article is not successful, the challenging party may request, within 30 days after receiving notification of the decision rejecting the challenge, the authority specified in clause 1 of Article 6 to decide on the challenge; such a decision shall be subject to no appeal; while such a request is pending, the arbitration tribunal, including the challenged arbitrator, may continue the arbitration proceedings and make an arbitration award. Failure to submit documents or appear at a hearing Unless otherwise agreed by the parties, if, without showing a sufficient cause: 1) the plaintiff fails to communicate its statement of claim under clause 1 of Article 23, the arbitration tribunal shall terminate the proceedings; 2) the defendant fails to communicate its statement of defence under clause 1 of Article 23, the arbitration tribunal shall continue the proceedings without treating such failure in itself as an admission of the plaintiff's allegations; 3) any party fails to appear at a hearing or to produce documentary evidence, the arbitration tribunal may continue the proceedings and make the award on the basis of evidence in the case; 4) any party fails to submit evidence at the request of the arbitration tribunal, the arbitration tribunal, depending on which person avoids its submission, as well as of what importance this evidence is, may recognise the circumstance for clarification whereof the evidence was to be submitted or refuse to recognise it or consider the case based on the evidence before it. An arbitration award may be set aside by the court specified in paragraph 2 of Article 6 only if: 1) the party making the application for setting aside furnishes proof that: a party to the arbitration agreement referred to in Article 7 was under some incapacity; or the said agreement is not valid under the law to which the parties have subjected it or, failing any indication thereon, under the law of Ukraine; or it was not given proper notice of the appointment of an arbitrator or the arbitration proceedings or was otherwise unable to present its case; or the award was made regarding a dispute not contemplated by or not falling within the terms of the arbitration agreement, or contains decisions on matters beyond the scope of the arbitration agreement, however, if the decisions on matters covered by the arbitration agreement can be separated from those not covered by such agreement, only that part of the award which contains decisions on matters not covered by such agreement may be set aside; or the composition of the arbitration tribunal or the arbitration procedure did not comply with the agreement of the parties unless such agreement was in conflict with any provision of this Law from which the parties cannot derogate, or, failing such agreement, did not comply with this Law; or 2) the court finds that: the subject matter of the dispute cannot be the object of the arbitration proceedings under the law Ukraine; or the arbitration award is contrary to the public policy of Ukraine. Recognition or enforcement of an arbitration award, irrespective of the country where it was made, may be refused only: 1) at the request of the party against which it is invoked, if that party furnishes to the competent court where recognition or enforcement is sought proof that: - the party to the arbitration agreement referred to in Article 7 was under some incapacity; or the said agreement is not valid under the law to which the parties have subjected it or, failing any indication thereon, under the law of the country where the award was made; or - the party against which the award is invoked was not given proper notice of the appointment of an arbitrator or the arbitration proceedings or was otherwise unable to present its case; or - the award was made regarding a dispute not contemplated by or not falling within the terms of the arbitration agreement, or contains decisions on matters beyond the scope of the arbitration agreement, however, if the decisions on matters covered by the arbitration agreement can be separated from those not covered by such agreement, only that part of the award which contains decisions on matters covered by such agreement may be recognised and enforced; or - the composition of the arbitration tribunal or the arbitration procedure did not comply with the agreement of the parties or, in the absence of such agreement, did not comply with the law of the country where the arbitration took place; or - the award has not yet become binding on the parties or has been set aside or suspended by a court of the state in which, or under the law of which, that award was made; or 2) if the court finds that: - the subject matter of the dispute is not capable of settlement by arbitration under the law Ukraine; or - the recognition or enforcement of the award is contrary to the public policy of Ukraine.
