This Law shall govern the proprietary and personal non-proprietary relations arising in connection with the acquisition, exercise and protection of the rights to plant varieties .<n>variety author – an individual who discovered or invented and improved the variety .<n>parent components – components of the first-generation hybrid (line, hybrid) that were used to create it .<n>State Party – a state that has acceded to the International Convention for the Protection of New Varieties of Plants . The legislation of Ukraine on the rights to plant varieties shall be based on the Constitution of Ukraine .<n>This Law applies to varieties of all genus and species of plants . The Regulatory Authority shall organise and carry out state supervision (oversight) in terms of protecting the rights to plant varieties .<n>The Competent Authority shall: ensure the development of scientific, material and technical potential in the field of protection of rights to plant varieties . The Competent Authority may authorise enterprises, institutions, organisations to exercise the powers in the field of rights to plant varieties assigned by this Law to the Competent Authority . Rights to plant varieties may be acquired: personal moral rights to a plant variety; intellectual property rights to a plant variety; intellectual property rights to the dissemination of plant varieties .<n>Expert institutions may not be authorised by the Competent Authority to exercise its powers regarding: decision-making on applications; issuance of security documents certifying rights to the variety . The scope of the legal protection of a variety for which a patent has been issued is determined by the set of features outlined in the description of the variety .<n>Various embodiments of a variety, to which rights may be acquired shall be a clone, line, first-generation hybrid and population . The term of validity of intellectual property rights to a variety, established by Article 39 of this Law, shall be reduced by the period calculated from the date of state registration of the variety to the date of state registration of rights to the variety . Rights to a variety shall be acquired in Ukraine by submitting an application with the Competence Authority .<n>Rights to a variety in foreign states shall be acquired independently of the acquisition of such rights in Ukraine .<n>The criteria for prohibiting the dissemination of varieties in Ukraine shall be devised by the Competent Authority . The right to file an application for a plant variety shall belong to the variety author unless otherwise specified by this Law or an agreement .<n>The author of a variety shall own the copyright, which shall be personal moral rights and are protected perpetually under Article 37 of this Law . If an employment agreement or the agreement of order between the employer and the breeder provides for the right to submit an application only by the employer, the breeder shall .<n>If an employer (successor of the employer) does not comply with the requirements specified in part 3 of this Article within the specified period or does not use the variety, keeping information about it confidential for more than four years from the date of receipt of the notice from the author then the right to file an application and obtain a patent passes to the author of the variety . An application for a variety shall be filed with the Competent Authority by a person entitled to do so .<n>If the applicant can be identified as a breeder based on an exchange of information between the Competent Authorities of the State Parties or the Member States of the International Union for the Protection of New Varieties of Plants, then an agreement on the transfer of the right to submit an application shall not be required . The application filing date shall be the date of receipt by the Competent Authority of the application materials .<n>If the Competent Authority considers that, at the time of receipt, the application materials do not meet the requirements of part 1 of this Article, it shall inform the applicant accordingly .<n>If the discrepancy is eliminated during this period of time, the date of submission of the application shall be the date of receipt of the corrected materials . An applicant may claim priority on the date of submission of a preliminary application for the same variety to the Competent Authority of another State Party .<n>An applicant may withdraw an application at any time until the date when the applicant receives a decision on the registration of a variety . The applicant shall have the right on his/her own initiative to make changes and clarifications to the application .<n>The Competent Authority or expert institution shall notify the applicant in writing of the results of the consideration of each individual motion received from the applicant . In case of receipt of materials on the basis of which the date of submission of the application cannot be established, the application is considered not submitted .<n>Where the formal characteristics of the application comply with the established requirements, an appropriate expert opinion shall be sent to the applicant .<n>The applicant shall remove the specified source of incompatibility within two months of the date of the receipt of expert opinion . The applicant shall have the right to apply to the Competent Authority or expert institution with a request to postpone the start of the substantive examination.<n>During the substantive examination, the Competent Authority or expert institution shall have the right to send the applicant a request for additional materials, information, documents, samples necessary to establish the novelty, distinctiveness, homogeneity and stability of the variety. A deadline missed for justifiable reasons shall be renewed, provided that the applicant submits a motion within 12 months from the date of its expiry and payment of the relevant fee .<n>If the Competent Authority establishes that the proposed name of a variety does not comply with the established requirements, an expert opinion shall be sent to the applicant requesting to propose another name for the variety . The name of a variety shall be approved by a decision on the emergence of rights to a variety .<n>The Competent Authority of each State Party shall be informed of any change of the variety name . Procedure established by the Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine .<n>An applicant may submit to the Competent Authority a reasoned objection to the registration of a variety, based on non-compliance with the requirements established by this Law .<n>An applicant may refute the objection, and leave the application unchanged, amend it or withdraw it . Application may be rejected if it does not meet the requirements of this Law .<n>If the declared variety does not meet the requirements of this Law, a decision shall be made to refuse the right to the variety .<n>The intellectual property right to the dissemination of a variety shall become effective from the date of payment of the fee for its emergence .<n>In case of non-payment of the State duty, the decision shall be revoked with respect to the emergence of intellectual property rights to the variety . State registration of intellectual property rights to a plant variety shall be carried out based on a decision on the state registration of rights to a variety .<n>The Register of Plant Varieties of Ukraine shall contain a set of official information on the state registration of intellectual property rights to the dissemination of plant varieties . The information entered in the registers shall be opened to the public .<n>The information and description of the variety entered in the Register of Plant Varieties of Ukraine and the Register of Patents shall be published in the official publication of the Competent Authority .<n>An applicant shall have the right to appeal against any decision, action or omission of the Competent Authority or expert institution regarding the application to the court . The right to the dissemination of a variety in Ukraine arise from the date of the decision on state registration of intellectual property rights to dissemination of the variety .<n>The owner of the intellectual property right to the dissemination of a variety shall have the right to transfer the right to the dissemination based on an agreement to any person . The patent owner may grant permission subject to certain conditions and restrictions .<n>A variety shall be considered to be substantially derived from another (original variety) if it is at least obviously distinguished from the original variety . The patent owner shall exercise his/her proprietary rights at his/her own discretion while proprietary rights of patent owners for other varieties should not be violated .<n>The patent owner shall have the right to transfer his/her proprietary rights to the variety based on an agreement to any person who shall become his/her successor .<n>The patent owner may grant permission (licence) for the use of a variety to any person based on a licence agreement . The term of validity of intellectual property rights to a plant variety shall begin on the day following the date of state registration of the right .<n>The transfer of intellectual property rights to a plant variety shall be considered valid if they are concluded in writing and signed by the parties . Fee for maintaining the validity of intellectual property rights to a plant variety .<n>Compulsory licences for the use of varieties may be granted by the Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine . A licensee shall pay a State duty for obtaining a compulsory licence .<n>The use of a variety without the consent of the patent owner shall not be recognised as an infringement of intellectual property rights to the plant variety . Intellectual property rights to a plant variety shall not apply to actions with respect to any material of its variety or variety .<n>Any person shall have the right to propagate, on his/her own farm and without being considered an infringement of the proprietary rights of the patent owner . The patent owner and the owner of the intellectual property right to the dissemination of a variety shall exercise their proprietary rights in good faith .<n>The patent owner and the owner of the intellectual property right to the dissemination of the variety shall ensure the preservation of the variety or its original components during the entire term of the patent . Any person using a planting material of a variety shall use the name of that variety even after the expiry of the term of its legal protection .<n>It is allowed to combine the variety name with signs for goods and services and with the indication of the origin of goods while using a variety .<n>The validity of intellectual property rights to a plant variety may be terminated early by the Competent Authority . The validity of intellectual property rights to a plant variety shall be terminated on the grounds specified in part 3 of this Article .<n>The dissemination of the variety shall be allowed until June 30 of the third year, calculating from the date of termination . The patent owner shall have the right to waive the intellectual property rights to a plant variety .<n>The waiver of rights and a patent shall become effective from the date of receipt by the Competent Authority .<n>If there is any valid licence agreement with the patent owner the waiver of intellectual property rights to the plant variety and a patent shall be possible only with the consent of the licensee . A person whose right to a variety has been infringed may apply to the court for the protection of such a right .<n>Disputes arising out of the relations regulated by this Law may be resolved in courts .<n>A person who has the right to use a variety subject to a licence agreement may also claim renewal of the infringed rights of the patent owner . Law provides for compensation for moral (non-proprietary) damage caused by infringement of rights to a variety .<n>Court may decide to impose a fine on the violator in the amount of 10 per cent of the amount awarded by the court in favour of the plaintiff .<n>The funds received from the payment of the State duty shall be used for implementation of the State policy in the field of protection of the rights to plant varieties . The state shall encourage the creation of new plant varieties .<n>Patents of Ukraine for plant varieties shall have equal legal status with the patents granted pursuant to this Law .<n>Within five years of the day when this Law enters into force, the genus and species of plant varieties to which the right of the variety owner extends may be restricted by the Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine .
