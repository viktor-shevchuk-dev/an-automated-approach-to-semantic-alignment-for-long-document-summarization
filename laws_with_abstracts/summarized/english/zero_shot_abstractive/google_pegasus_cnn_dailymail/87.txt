This Law is based on the recognition of the value of arbitration (arbitration tribunal) as a widely used method of settling disputes .<n>This Law applies to international commercial arbitration if the place of arbitration is located within the territory of Ukraine . ‘Court’ means a body of the judicial system of the state .<n>Relationships of commercial nature include, but are not limited to, the following agreements .<n>The provisions of this Article do not apply to communications in court proceedings . Authorities for certain functions of arbitration assistance and supervision .<n>An arbitration agreement may be concluded in the form of an arbitration clause in a contract or in the form of a separate agreement . Where an action referred to in clause 1 of this Article has been brought, arbitration proceedings may nevertheless be commenced or continued .<n>An arbitration award may be made, while the issue of jurisdiction is pending before the court .<n>No person shall be precluded by reason of his or her nationality from acting as an arbitrator unless otherwise agreed by the parties . A party may challenge an arbitrator appointed by it or whose appointment it has participated in, only for reasons that has become known to it after the appointment.<n>If a challenge under any procedure agreed upon by the parties or under the procedure of clause 2 of this Article is not successful, the challenging party may request, within 30 days after receiving notification of the decision rejecting the challenge.<n>While such a request is pending, the arbitration tribunal, including the challenged arbitrator, may continue the arbitration proceedings and make an arbitration award. A party is not precluded from raising a plea by the fact that it has appointed, or participated in the appointment of, an arbitrator .<n>A plea that the arbitration tribunal does not have jurisdiction shall be raised not later than the submission of the statement of defence .<n>The arbitration tribunal may, in either case, admit a later plea if it considers the delay justified . The arbitration tribunal may rule on a plea referred to in clause 2 of this Article either as a preliminary issue or in an award on the merits .<n>If the arbitration tribunal rules as a preliminary issue that it has jurisdiction, any party may request, within 30 days after having received notification of that ruling .<n>While such a request is pending, the arbitration tribunal may continue the arbitration proceedings and make an arbitration award . The parties are free to agree on the language or languages to be used in the arbitration proceedings .<n>The arbitration tribunal may order that any documentary evidence shall be accompanied by a translation . The arbitration tribunal shall decide on the dispute under such rules of law as are chosen by the parties as applicable to the merits of the dispute .<n>In the absence of any designation by the parties, the arbitration tribunal shall apply the law determined by the conflict-of-law rules which it considers applicable . In arbitration proceedings with a panel of arbitrators, any decision of the arbitration tribunal shall be made, unless otherwise agreed by the parties, by a majority of all its members .<n>An arbitration award on agreed terms shall be made under the provisions of Article 31 and shall state that it is an arbitration award .<n>The arbitration tribunal shall issue an order for the termination of the arbitration proceedings when: - the plaintiff withdraws its claim unless the defendant objects thereto and the arbitration tribunal recognises a legitimate interest on his part in obtaining a final settlement of the dispute . The mandate of the arbitration tribunal terminates with the termination of the arbitration proceedings without prejudice to the provisions of Article 33 and clause 4 of Article 34.<n>The arbitration tribunal may correct any errors referred to in paragraph 2 of clause 1 of this Article on its own initiative within 30 days of the date of the arbitration award.<n>An arbitration award may be set aside by the court specified in paragraph 2 of Article 6 only if: 1) the party making the application for setting aside furnishes proof that: a party to the arbitration agreement referred to in Article 7 was under some incapacity. An arbitration award, irrespective of the country where it was made, shall be recognised as binding .<n>An application for setting aside an arbitration award may not be made after three months have elapsed from the date on which the party making that application had received the arbitration award . This Law shall apply to the procedure for resolving disputes in arbitration provided for by the Law of Ukraine "On Financial Restructuring"<n>If an application for setting aside or suspension of an arbitration award has been made to a court referred to in paragraph 5, subclause 1, clause 1 of this Article, the court may, if it considers it proper, adjourn its decision and may also, on the application of the party claiming recognition or enforcement of the arbitration award, order the other party to provide appropriate security. The International Commercial Arbitration Court is an independent, permanently functioning arbitration institution (arbitration tribunal)<n>The Ukrainian Chamber of Commerce and Industry approves the Rules of the International Commercial Arbitration Court . The Maritime Arbitration Commission is an independent, permanently functioning arbitration institution (arbitration tribunal)<n>The Ukrainian Chamber of Commerce and Industry approves the Rules of the Maritime Arbitration Commission . Maritime Arbitration Commission at the Ukrainian Chamber of Commerce and Industry has its own seal reproducing its name in the Ukrainian and English languages and displaying an anchor and the scales of justice.
