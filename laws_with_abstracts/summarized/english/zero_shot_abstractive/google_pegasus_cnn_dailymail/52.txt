Family Code of Ukraine defines principles of marriage, personal non-property and property rights and responsibilities of spouses .<n>The Family Code of Ukraine governs personal proprietary and non-proprietary relations between grandparents, great-grandparents and grandchildren, great-grandchildren, siblings, step-parents and step-children .<n>The Family Code of Ukraine does not govern family relations between cousins, uncles, aunts, nephews, nieces and other relatives by origin . In cases referred to by Part 2 of Article 23, a family may be started by a person under the age of marriage .<n>A family may be started by a person who gave birth to a child, regardless of their age .<n>Everyone has the right to live with their family . Family relations shall be governed to cater for the needs of the child and incapacitated family members .<n>Family relations shall be governed under the principles of justice, good faith and reasonability .<n>Every participant of family relations may seek protection in court . Family rights are closely associated with the person and therefore may not be assigned to any other person .<n>Family duties are closely associated with the person and therefore may not be assigned to any other person .<n>Each participant of family relations who has attained the age of 14 may directly go to court for the protection of their right or interest . In cases referred to in this Code the person may request the guardianship agency to support their family rights and interests .<n>A guardianship agency shall submit to the court a written opinion on the dispute resolution based on the feedback from the survey of the living conditions of the child .<n>The court may disagree with the opinion of the guardianship agency if it is insufficiently motivated or is in conflict with the child's interests . Marriage is a family union of a woman and a man duly registered with the state civil registration office .<n>Neither the woman nor the man shall be forced to marry .<n>A woman and a man may be in one marriage at a time only .<n>Full and half siblings may not marry each other . Application for marriage registration shall be filed by the woman and the man in person .<n>If the marriage registration did not take place on the designated day, the marriage registration application shall become null and void three months upon the submission .<n>The partner who refused to marry must reimburse the other partner any and all costs and expenses incurred in preparation for the marriage registration and wedding . Marriage may not be grounds for any benefits or preference or any restrictions of any rights and freedoms protected under the Constitution and applicable laws of Ukraine .<n>The couple may opt to choose last name of one of the partners as their mutual last name or not to change their last names after marriage .<n>Marriage shall be deemed lawful except where otherwise governed by parts 1 to 3 of Article 39 of this Code and if not otherwise declared null and void by the court . A marriage that is entered into by a person who is already in a registered marriage shall be void .<n>A marriage registered with a person declared incapable shall be void .<n>A sham marriage shall be annulled by the court . When awarding a judgment on the marriage annulment the court shall also consider the extent to which the marriage has infringed any rights and interests of the person .<n>A marriage may not be declared void if the wife is pregnant or in case of childbirth to the persons referred to in Clauses 1, 2 and 4, Part 1 of this Article .<n>In the cases referred to in Article 39 to Article 41 of this Code the marriage shall be deemed void as of the day of its state registration . A marriage registered in absence of either partner shall be deemed null and void .<n>The husband's unwillingness or inability to have a child may be the grounds for termination of the marriage .<n>The pregnant wife shall enjoy an environment in the family that is good for her health and giving birth to a healthy child . All essential family matters shall be decided by spouses jointly according to the principles of equality .<n>All actions taken by a spouse regarding the family life shall be deemed to be committed upon consent of the other spouse .<n>Wife and husband may choose their place of residence at their own discretion . Wife and husband may take any action not explicitly prohibited by law or not otherwise in conflict with moral principles of the society to maintain their marriage.<n>Either spouse may choose to terminate the marriage.<n>Forcing to terminate or stay in marriage, or forcing to have sex through physical or psychological violence, is an abuse of the right of wife or husband to freedom and personal inviolability. Property acquired by the spouses in marriage shall be jointly owned by the wife and the husband .<n>Every item acquired in marriage other than individual use items shall be deemed to be jointly owned by the couple .<n>Salary, pension, stipend and any other income obtained by either spouse shall be joint property .<n>In case either spouse executed an agreement to serve the interests of the family, then any money or assets, including any fees or award being the proceeds of this agreement, shall be jointly owned by the spouses . Wife and husband may enter into any agreements not explicitly prohibited by law .<n>The spouses may agree on how any property jointly owned by them will be used .<n>Right of the spouses to decide on the property use . Spouses may split the property in their joint ownership regardless of the marriage dissolution .<n>Dispute related to the division of the marital assets may divert from the principle of equality of the spousal shares .<n>The court may award that the share of the property owned by the wife/husband be increased if such spouse lives with the children . If the spouses have not reached agreement on how to split the assets, the dispute may be resolved in court .<n>Items that cannot be divided shall be awarded to either spouse unless otherwise agreed between them .<n>Financial compensation may be awarded to the relevant spouse if the other spouse credits the relevant amount to the deposit account of the court in advance . Spousal maintenance after divorce shall not terminate the eligibility to maintenance that emerged during the marriage .<n>A spouse shall be deemed to be in need of maintenance, if their salary, pension, property proceeds or any other income are below the subsistence level established by law .<n>The spouse who became incapacitated due to abuse by another spouse shall be entitled to maintenance regardless of the right to compensation for damage contemplated by the Civil Code of Ukraine . Alimony awarded by court shall commence to accrue as of the day when the claim was filed with such court .<n>If either spouse receives alimony due to their disability, alimony shall continue to be paid until such disability persists . Right of either spouse to financial support, including to post-divorce spousal maintenance, shall terminate upon the recovery of their capacity and registration of a new marriage .<n>All money received as alimony shall be deemed to have been received without due legal grounds .<n>Right of either spouse to the court-ordered alimony may be terminated by the court if it is found that: 1) the recipient is no longer in need of material support; 2) the payer is not capable of providing material support . The wife's right to maintenance shall terminate if the child is born dead, given to another person for raising, or in case of the child's death .<n>The husband's right to maintenance shall terminate if his wife's name as the mother was removed from the child's birth registration record .<n>The wife's right to maintenance shall terminate if her husband's name as the father was removed from the child's birth registration record . Spouses and ex-spouses may enter into a maintenance termination agreement in lieu of a title to a residential house, apartment or another real estate, or a one-off payment .<n>Right to maintenance shall be terminated on the grounds referred to in Part 2 and Part 4 of Article 83 as well as Article 85, Article 87 and Article 89 of this Code . A marriage contract may not affect the scope of rights of the child defined by this Code .<n>Real estate or any other assets title to which is subject to state registration may not be transferred to either spouse under the marriage contract . The parties may agree to reside in a residential property owned by either spouse or jointly by both spouses or by any of their relatives .<n>The marriage contract may be terminated if so decided by the competent court in connection with any reasons of essence .<n>The marriage contract may be declared by a competent court as null and void due to the applicable provisions envisaged in the Civil Code of Ukraine . Marriage shall be terminated by virtue of dissolution upon an application for divorce filed by both spouses .<n>Marriage shall be terminated regardless of any property disputes between the spouses .<n>On the basis of an application filed by an interested person in accordance with Article 106 of this Code, a divorce may be declared a sham divorce . Application for a divorce may not be filed during the wife's pregnancy .<n>The husband/wife may apply for a divorce before the child reaches the age of one year .<n>The court may grant a divorce if it has established that living as a family and saving the marriage would be in conflict with the essential interests of either spouse or the interests of their children .<n>The person who changed their last name following the marriage registration may choose to continue using that last name or revert to their pre-marriage last name . Divorce awarded in accordance with Article 106 and Article 107 of this Code must be registered with the state civil registration office .<n>A person may marry again following the marriage termination certified by the Divorce Certificate .<n>If the person presumed dead has been found to be alive and the relevant court judgment has been cancelled, their marriage with the other spouse may be restored . The child who was conceived and/or born during the marriage shall be the child of the spouses.<n>The child born within ten months following the marriage termination of annulment shall be deemed to be the child of the spouses.<n>The spouses shall be recognised as the parents of the child born to the wife upon the transfer of the human embryo conceived by her husband and another woman with the use of auxiliary reproductive technology. When the child's mother and father are not married to each other, paternity shall be established on the basis of the application of the child's mother and father .<n>If no application referred to in Article 126 of this Code has been submitted, paternity regarding the child may be recognised by the court .<n>A paternity recognition claim may be submitted by the child's mother, custodian, caregiver, or the child themselves upon attaining the age of majority . A person who claims to be the child's mother may file an application to establish their maternity .<n>In case of death of a woman who claimed to be the child's mother her maternity may be established by court .<n>If the child was born in wedlock, the wife shall be registered as the mother and the husband shall be registered as the father . Paternity may only be challenged upon the birth of the child and before the child attains the age of majority .<n>Paternity may not be challenged after the death of the person registered as the child's father . If the person registered as the child's father died before the child's birth, his paternity may be challenged by their heirs .<n>A woman who gave birth to a child in wedlock may challenge the paternity of her husband .<n>A claim submitted by the mother to get the name of her husband as the child's father removed from the child's birth registration record may be satisfied only if the paternity is claimed by another person . The mother and the father of the child who are married to each other must take the child from the maternity hospital or other health care facility .<n>The father who is not married to the child's mother and whose name appears on the child's birth certificate as the father or whose paternity is recognised by the court, must, when given a notice by the child care authorities .<n>The parents must immediately, but in any case not later than one months following the child's birth date, get the child's birth registered with the state civil registration office . In case of death of the parents or where they are otherwise unable to register the child's birth, the child's birth shall be registered .<n>In the absence of voluntary paternity recognition, the name of the child born to a woman who is not married shall be given by the child's mother .<n>The child's patronymic shall derive from the first name of their father . In the event that either parent changes their last name the last name of the child may be changed subject to the consent of both parents and the child .<n>The child who has reached the age of sixteen may at their own discretion change their first name and/or last name and/or patronymic by the child .<n>The child who has reached the age of fourteen may change their patronymic with the consent of their parents . All forms of child exploitation by the parents shall be prohibited .<n>The parents shall have a priority over other persons for personal rearing of their child .<n>The parents may apply to the court, state authorities, local governments and NGOs for the protection of the rights and interests of the child . Parental rights may not be exercised in conflict with the child's interests .<n>Rejection of the child by the parents is illegitimate .<n>Evasion of parental obligation may lead to being hold liable . The parent who was awarded to be the resident parent by the court judgment or opinion of the guardianship agency shall, at their own discretion, resolve any matters related to travelling outside Ukraine for one month and longer .<n>The child support arrears certificate shall be issued by the state executive office in accordance with the applicable law .<n>If the resident parent does not give a consent certified by a notary for the child to travel abroad for the designated purpose within ten days upon receiving the notice by certified mail, the non-resident parent, provided that they do not have any child support payments in arrears, may request the court to award the permission for the child to travel abroad without the consent of the other parent . The guardianship agency shall determine the forms of participation in the child-rearing for the non-resident parent .<n>The person who is evading from complying with the decision of the guardianship agency must reimburse the material and moral damage sustained by the non-resident parent . Place of residence of the child who has reached the age of ten shall be determined mutually by the parents and the child .<n>In case the parents live separately, the child who has reached the age of fourteen shall determine their place of residence at their own discretion . Parents may demand taking the minor away from any person who keeps the minor with them other than under the applicable law or a court award .<n>The court may terminate parental rights of either parent if such parent: 1) did not take the child from the maternity hospital or any other healthcare facility without a valid reason and was not providing any parental care to the child within six months; 2) evades their duties regarding the child's upbringing and/or their complete general secondary education; 3) is abusing the child; 4) is a chronic alcoholic or drug addict; 5) exploits the child in any way, including forcing the child into begging and vagrancy .<n>The mother/father may be deprived of parental rights pertinent to all of their children or some of them. Person whose parental rights were terminated shall: 1) lose any and all personal non-property rights related to the child; 2) cease to be the legitimate representative of the child; 3) lose the right to any benefits and state support provided to families with children; 4) not be allowed to be an adoptive parent, custodian or caregiver .<n>If the child used to live with the parent whose parental rights are terminated, the court shall investigate whether they can continue living in the same residential property . If the child cannot be given to the grandparents, adult siblings, other relatives of the child including step-parents, they shall be given to the guardianship agency .<n>The child who has been given to their relatives, step-parents or the guardianship agency shall have the right to continue living in the residential property where they used to live in . The court may award an order to take the child away from the parent(s) without terminating their parental rights .<n>In exceptional cases, the guardianship agency or the prosecutor may award an order to take the child away from the parents immediately .<n>The child shall be given to the other parent, grandparent(s) or other relatives who intend to do so, or the guardianship agency . Separate property of parents and children .<n>Rights of the parents and children to use the property .<n>The parents must take care of the child's property for the sake of the child's interests . If the minor is capable of defining their needs and interests, the parents shall manage their property in line with such needs and interests .<n>The parents may give consent to the underage child's execution of any transactions only if approved by the guardianship agency .<n>The guardianship agency shall approve any transactions related to the child's real estate only upon a one-month review procedure . When parents are no longer managing the child's property, they must return the property, including any proceeds deriving from it, to the child .<n>The parents must provide for the child until the child reaches the age of majority .<n>The underage child may receive and administer the child support payments at their own discretion . In case either parent moves abroad for permanent residence in a country with which Ukraine does not have a legal aid agreement in place child support shall be collected .<n>If the place of residence or location of the parents is unknown, or they evade paying the child support, or are unable to provide for the child, the child shall be receiving temporary state support .<n> Provisional state allowance shall be allocated from the State Budget of Ukraine . Minimum guaranteed amount of child support per one child must be at least 50% of the subsistence wage for a child of the relevant age .<n>The court shall not be limited by the amount of the payer's income if it has been established that the payer's costs and expenses exceed their income . The court shall determine the child support as a fixed amount .<n>The parent who was awarded to pay the child support, and the other parent who was not awarded to pay the child support shall share any additional expenses for the child caused by any special circumstances .<n>Additional expenses associated with the child may be covered in advance or as made; as a one-off payment, periodical payment or on a permanent basis . In case child support spending is not in line with the intended purpose, the payer may apply to the court to reduce the amount of alimony or transfer a certain part of child support to the child's personal account with the State Savings Bank of Ukraine .<n>The parents may enter into the child support agreement that would govern the amount and timings for such payments .<n>Terms and conditions of the agreement may not infringe any rights of the child protected under this Code . If either parent fails to meet their alimony obligation under the child support agreement, child support may be collected under an executive order of the notary .<n>The title to the real estate shall be vested in the child or the child and the resident parent as joint owners of this property .<n>The property received by the child under this agreement may be assigned before the child reaches the age of majority . Collection of child support and other forms of maintenance for the child who is in a health care facility, educational facility or any other facility .<n>Child support may be collected retroactively under the executive order provided .<n>Child support arrears shall be paid regardless of whether the child has reached the age of majority . Law on child support arrears and liability for failure to pay on time and making additional payments for the child .<n>Child support arrears of the payer who was unemployed as of the time the arrears arose .<n>Child support arrears of the payer who is making income in a country with which Ukraine does not have a legal aid agreement in place .<n>No late payment fine shall accrue if the payer is underage . The court may postpone or extend the period for payment of child support arrears with due regard to the payee's financial and marital status.<n>The court shall determine the amount of child support for the adult daughter/son as a fixed amount and as a share of the payer's income with due regard of the circumstances referred to in Article 182 of this Code. The court may release the daughter/son from the duty to provide for the mother/father if it has been established that the mother/father were evading from the parental duties .<n>In exceptional cases the court may award an order for the daughter/son to pay maintenance for the period that would be no longer than three years . Adoption is taking by the adoptive parent of a person to the adoptive parent's family as the daughter/son .<n>In exceptional cases the court may award the adoption of an adult person who does not have a mother/father .<n>The adoptive parent must be at least fifteen years older than the child to be adopted . Number of children that may be adopted by an adoptive parent is not limited .<n>If the child has mother only, the child may not be adopted by the person who is not married to their mother .<n>If the child has father only, the child may not be adopted by the person who is not married to their father . Other persons whose interests are in conflict with interests of the child may not become adoptive parents .<n>If several persons are willing to adopt the same child, the priority shall have the citizen of Ukraine who meets the following criteria . Any mediation or commercial services in relation to adoption, custody, guardianship or foster care of children in families of Ukrainian or foreign nationals shall be prohibited .<n> Consent of the parents to adoption of the child must be unconditional .<n>If the child's mother/father is underage, adoption also requires the relevant consent of their parents . The child may be adopted without consent of the parents, if the parents: 1) are unknown; 2) were declared missing; 3) were declared incapable; 4) were deprived of parental rights in regard of the child being adopted; 5) within two months following the childbirth did not take the child to the family and the record about them in the Birth Registration Book is made in accordance with Article 135 of this Code.<n>The child may be adopted without the consent of the adult parents if the court established that the parents have not been living together with the child for over six months without any valid reasons, and do not provide parental care and support to the child. The adoption application may be revoked pending the entry into force of the court judgment awarding the adoption .<n>The child may not reject adoption on the grounds that the person already has a child or may give birth to a child .<n>The adoptive parent shall have the right to conceal the fact of adoption from the adopted child . Secrecy of adoption shall be protected in accordance with Article 229 to 231 of this Code .<n>Right of the adoptive parent to be registered as the child's mother/father .<n>Change of first name, last name and patronymic of the adopted person . Where the person who has reached the age of majority is adopted, their first name, last name and patronymic may be changed .<n>The court shall indicate the changes envisaged by this Article in the adoption award . The adopted child retains their right to pension, other social welfare options and damage compensation for the loss of the caregiver .<n>The guardianship agency shall oversee the protection of rights of the adopted child .<n>The court shall declare adoption void if it took place without the consent of the child and the parents . Adoption may not be annulled after the child reaches the age of majority .<n> Adoption shall be annulled as of the day when the court judgment becomes effective . Following adoption annulment the child may continue to have the first name, last name and patronymic they received upon being adopted .<n>The child may return the first name, last name and patronymic they had prior to being adopted .<n>Parents, adoptive parent, guardian, caregiver, guardianship agency, prosecutor or the adopted child themselves upon reaching the age of fourteen may apply to the court seeking adoption to be annulled or declared void . Article 245. Custody and guardianship of the child residing in a healthcare facility, educational setting or any other child care institution .<n>Guardianship or custodianship shall not terminate the child's right to receive pension, support, compensation of damage in connection with the loss of the caregiver .<n>The orphaned child and the child deprived of parental care living in a healthcare facility, educational setting, other child care centre or an adoptive family . Civil rights and obligations of the guardian/ caregiver shall be governed by the Civil Code of Ukraine .<n>Guardianship/custodianship shall terminated in the cases referred to by the Civil Code of Ukraine .<n>Only the children who are full siblings or who were brought up in the same family may be placed concurrently in a foster family . Procedure for creating a foster family and placement of the child in a foster family shall be approved by the Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine .<n>The child's consent is required for placing the child in a foster family if the child has reached the age and stage of development to be able to express such consent .<n>While the child is staying in a foster family, they shall retain their right to alimony, pension, social allowance and compensation in case of loss of caregiver . Adoptive parents have obligations regarding upbringing and development of the children .<n>Adopted children are orphan children and children deprived of parental care adopted by the adoptive family .<n>The child's consent to be placed in a foster family shall be given to an official staff member of the facility where the child is staying, in presence of the adoptive parents and a representative of the guardianship agency . Orphan children and children deprived of parental care who have disabilities may, at their own discretion, continue to live in the adoptive family until they reach the age of twenty-three .<n>The adopted children shall retain their right to alimony, pension, other social benefits and compensation for the loss of caregiver that they had prior to the adoption .<n>The regulations on the adoptive family and standard adoption agreement shall be approved by the Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine . Orphan children and children deprived of parental care who have disabilities may, at their own discretion, continue to live in the foster home until they reach the age of twenty-three .<n>The foster care alumni shall retain their right to alimony, pension, other social benefits and compensation for the loss of caregiver that they had prior to the admission to the foster home . The grandparents/great-grandparents may communicate with and get involved in raising the grandchildren and great-grandchildren .<n>The siblings shall have the rights and obligations envisaged by the applicable law for the siblings .<n>The sibling and step-parent shall have the right to protect their minor, underage and adult incapable siblings and step-children . disputes related to the participation of the grandparent, great-grandparent, sibling or step-parent in raising the child shall be resolved by the court under Article 159 of this Code .<n>The grandchildren and great-grandchildren must provide care to their grandparents and great-grandparents .<n>The adult siblings must provide for the incapable grandparents/great-grandparents who are in need of financial support . The court may release the step-parent from the obligation to provide for the step-child or limit the duration of such obligation .<n>The obligation arises when the person who is in need of financial support does not have spouse/adult children/siblings or where these persons for valid reasons are not able to provide them with sufficient support .<n>The amount of maintenance collected from other family members and relatives for the benefit of the children and incapable adult persons who are in need of financial assistance shall be set as a share of income or as a fixed amount . The total amount of maintenance to be collected must be at least 30% of the subsistence wage for a child of the relevant age .<n>A child who is a citizen of Ukraine but resides outside Ukraine shall be adopted by a citizen of Ukraine at a consular section or a diplomatic mission of Ukraine .<n>If the adoptive parent is not a citizen of Ukraine, adoption of the child who is a citizen of Ukraine requires the consent of the central executive agency . The child may be adopted by a foreign national unless a citizen of Ukraine volunteered to adopt or foster this child .<n>The adopted child shall remain the citizen of Ukraine until they reach the age of eighteen . Adoption of a child who is a foreign national or a stateless person by a foreign national in Ukraine shall be under the applicable laws of Ukraine unless otherwise governed by the international treaties of Ukraine .<n>In case the children are adopted by foreign nationals and reside outside Ukraine, the relevant consular section shall, upon assignment by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Ukraine, register these children and supervise that they rights are duly respected until they reach the age of eighteen .<n> Procedure for supervision for the protection of rights of the children adopted by foreign nationals and residing outside Ukraine shall be set out by the Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine . During the lockdown introduced by the Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine to prevent the spread of the coronavirus disease (COVID-19), the periods referred to in Article 72, Article 128, Article 129 and Article 139 of this Code shall be extended for the duration of such lockdown.<n>The Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine shall, within three months upon the publication of this Code, submit its proposals on amendments to the Laws of Ukraine deriving from this Code to the Verkhovna Rada of Ukraine.
