this Law is aimed at ensuring the attraction and efficient allocation of financial resources by the investors . it defines the legal and organisational basis for the establishment, operation and termination of collective investment entities . collective investment activity shall mean activities carried out for the benefit of the participants (participant) of the collective investment institution . collective investment institution shall mean a corporate or unit fund . control shall mean ownership, directly or through related individuals and/or legal entities . holding of posts of head, deputy head, members of the governing bodies or executive bodies by persons who already hold one or more of the mentioned posts in another legal entity . collective investment funds shall mean funds contributed by the founders of the corporate fund . related persons shall mean legal entities and/or individuals whose relationship may have an impact on the conditions or economic results of their activities . family members of an individual shall be considered to be his or her spouse, direct relatives (children or parents) the legislation on joint-stock companies shall not apply to the regulation of the activities of corporate funds . the custodian of the assets of the collective investment institution, the depository, the auditor (audit firm), the property appraiser may not be participants . an individual may be a participant in a venture capital fund provided that the securities thereof are purchased in an amount equal to or greater than 1,500 times the monthly minimum wage . collective investment institutions shall be registered by the Commission by entering information on the collective investment institution in the Register and assigning the registration code to that institution . the grounds for refusing to register the rules shall be: 1) incompliance with the submitted documents with the requirements of the law; 2) absence of documents required by law; 3) false information in the documents submitted. notice of refusal to register the rules and to enter a collective investment institution in the Register shall be sent to the applicant by a letter signed by the authorised official of the Commission . if the applicant takes full account of the observations of the commission, the Commission may not refuse to register . collective investment activities shall be carried out after the collective investment institutions are entered in the register and the Certificate of Entry into the Register obtained . a collective investment institution shall be established for a fixed period, as specified in its rules . the operating term of the fixed-term corporate fund may be extended by a resolution . in the event of an extension of the term, the participants must redeem their securities . the collective investment institution shall be considered specialised if it invests exclusively in assets defined hereby . only diversified, specialised and qualified investment institutions may be regarded the interval collective investment institutions . the securities of such an institution are subject to compulsory circulation on the stock exchange . non-diversified closed collective investment institution, which carries out exclusively private placement of its securities among legal entities and individuals shall be regarded a venture fund . a corporate fund is a legal entity established in the form of a joint-stock company . the corporate fund and its bodies may not be subject to any sanctions restricting their rights . participants of a corporate fund shall not be liable for the obligations of the corporate fund . participants of corporate funds whose assets comprise above 10 per cent of the shares of the bank are liable to obligations of bank under law . corporate fund acquired status of collective investment institution from the day it is entered into the Register . a corporate fund may not be established by a legal entity with a state or communal share in their authorised capital of more than 25 per cent . the founders of the corporate fund shall pay for the shares of the fund with the funds only . a breach of the establishment phases of the corporate fund shall be regarded as a ground for the Commission to refuse issuing a certificate . the number of votes of the founders at the founding meeting of a corporate fund is determined by the amount of its shares acquired by each of its founders . a report on the results of private placement of shares among founders must be registered within 30 working days . founding meeting of a corporate fund shall decide on the charter of the fund . resolutions on any other matters shall be taken by a simple majority of the founders . charter should contain information on: 1) Full name of the corporate fund in Ukrainian; 2) Type of corporate fund (diversified, non-diversified, specialised, qualified); 3) Investment fund class if corporate fund is specialised or qualified . if the charter is not approved, funds shall be returned within 30 working days . charter of the corporate fund may not grant additional rights or powers to its founders over other participants in such a corporate fund . corporate fund shall be obliged to register its rules within six months from the date of the state registration of corporate fund as a legal entity . rules shall contain information on: 1) Full name of the corporation; 2) Identification code of the company according to the unified state register of legal entities . the rules and amendments thereto shall be approved by the supervisory board of the corporate fund . the authorised capital of a corporate fund shall constitute no less than 1250 times the minimum monthly wage established by law on the registration date of the fund as a legal entity . the general meeting and the supervisory board shall be the bodies of the corporate fund . the annual general meeting shall be held not later than 30 April of the year following the reporting year . all other general meetings, except for annual meetings, are considered to be extraordinary . the supervisory board of a corporate fund may not be granted with the powers to decide matters falling within the exclusive competence of the general meeting . a general meeting may adopt resolutions on the issues of holding the meeting, unless otherwise provided for in the charter or rules . the list of participants of the corporate fund entitled to participate in the general meeting shall be compiled by the depository of securities at the end of the operational day three working days before the day of the meeting . a corporate fund or a person keeping a record of the ownership of shares in such a fund shall be obliged to provide information on its inclusion in the list . the written notice on holding a general meeting and its agenda shall be sent personally by the person convening the meeting, at least 30 calendar days prior to the day corporate fund with more than 1,000 participants shall publish a notice on holding the general meeting in the official printed media of the Commission . the corporate fund having shares traded on the stock exchange shall send the notice to such a stock exchange . a corporate fund may not amend documents made available to participants of corporate fund or that they had the opportunity to review . each participant of the corporate fund has the right to make proposals on matters included in the agenda of the general meeting . the number of such proposals may not exceed the composition of the supervisory board . proposals by the participants (participant) collectively owning 5 per cent or more of the shares of the corporation fund shall be mandatory included on the agenda . a resolution not to include a proposal by a participant of a corporate fund may be adopted . an appeal filed to the court by the participant of the corporate fund, against the resolution by the supervisory board, shall not cause the suspension of the general meeting . the authorisation of the representative to participate and vote in the general meeting shall not exclude the participation of the participant of the corporate fund, having issued the power of attorney, instead of his/her representative . when voting, the representative must vote strictly as specified in the task of voting, taking into account the interests of the person he/she represents . the registration commission shall have the right to refuse registration to the participant of the corporate fund (his/her representative) only in the absence of the documents of the participant, whereby he is authorised to participate in the general meeting in accordance with the law . the powers of the registry commission may be assigned under the agreement to the depository or the asset management company . participants of the corporate fund and the Commission may appoint representatives to supervise registration of participants . the process of the general meeting or the consideration of a separate matter by resolution may be recorded with technical means . resolutions passed with a simple majority of votes shall be passed unless otherwise stipulated herein . the charter of the corporate fund may establish a higher number of votes required for adopting resolutions on the agenda items . members of the supervisory board shall be elected by a cumulative vote . the resolution of the general meeting on the liquidation of the fixed-term corporate fund before the expiry of the deadline set by the rules shall be adopted by more than three quarters . a bullet must be signed by the participant (representative of the participant) of the corporate fund . in the absence of such signature, be considered invalid; 6) Number of votes held . the form and text of the ballot shall be approved by the supervisory board . a cumulative ballot shall be declared invalid if a participant has indicated on the ballot a higher number of votes than he holds . the Counting commission, which is elected by the general meeting, shall provide clarification on the voting, counting and other matters related to ensuring that the voting takes place . the minutes of the general meeting shall be drawn up within 10 working days of the closure of a general meeting . an extraordinary general meeting may be convened by the supervisory board . the minutes shall be bound, sealed and signed by the chairperson of the supervising board of the corporate fund . a request to convene an extraordinary general meeting shall be submitted in writing . the supervisory board shall pass a resolution convening or refusing the meeting . an extraordinary meeting shall take place within 15 working days of the resolution . resolution of the supervisory board not to convene an extraordinary general meeting may be appealed to the court by the participants of the corporate fund . resolutions of the general meeting on all matters falling within the exclusive competence of the gmt may be adopted by polling method . a notice of such a meeting and other material shall be sent to all participants of a corporate fund at the expense of such participants . resolution to hold the general meeting by polling method must be published in the official printed media of the Commission at least 20 working days before the set date of completion of the receipt of the ballots by the corporate fund . in the absence of certification, the signature of a corporate fund participant, being an individual, shall be considered invalid . the ballot to vote by polling method shall be signed by the chairperson of the supervisory board of the corporate fund . the signature of participant of corporate fund on the ballot may be certified by the depositary, custodian, notary and other notaries and any other officers performing notarial acts or otherwise as provided by law . if the signature is not certified the ballot shall be considered invalid . processing of voting ballots by polling method and counting of the votes fund shall not commence before the set date of the end of receipt by the corporate fund . the participants (participant) of corporate fund shall have the right to control the counting of votes in accordance with its established procedure . the voting results by polling method shall be communicated to the participants of the corporate fund in a time and manner determined by the charter or by the regulation on the general meeting . if a corporate fund consists of one person, the provisions of Articles 19 to 31 hereof shall not apply to such a fund . resolutions of the participant of the corporate fund on matters falling within the competence of the supervisory board shall be made by him in writing (in the form of a resolution, an order, etc.) and certified by the seal of the corporation fund or notary . The Supervisory Board shall be responsible for: 1) Adoption of resolution to hold ordinary and extraordinary general meetings . a meeting of the supervisory board shall be convened at the initiative of the chairperson . insolvency of the corporate fund; a decrease in the value of net assets by more than 25 per cent compared to the results of the last appraisal . the supervisory board shall be entitled to the meeting if at least half of its members participate . the charter may establish a larger number of members for the recognition of its meetings as competent .
