3. The acquisition of the right to a trademark shall be certified by a certificate. The validity period of a certificate shall be ten years from the date of submitting an application to NIPO and may be extended by NIPO at the request of the certificate holder every ten years, provided that the fee is paid under the procedure established by clause 2, Article 18 of this Law. The procedure for extending the validity period of the certificate shall be determined by the central executive authority in charge of shaping and implementing state policy in the field of intellectual property.
