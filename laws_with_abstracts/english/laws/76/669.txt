Article 143. Territorial communities of a village, settlement, and city shall, directly or through the local government established by them, manage the communal property; approve programmes of socio-economic and cultural development and control their implementation; approve budgets of respective administrative and territorial units and control the execution of such budgets; establish local taxes and duties in accordance with the law; ensure holding of local referendums and the implementation of their results; establish, reorganise and liquidate communal enterprises, organisations and institutions and exercise control over their activities; settle other local issues assigned to their competence by law.
