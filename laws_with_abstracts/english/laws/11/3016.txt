3. The court shall have the right, where the participants to court proceedings do not object thereto, to find that examination of evidence in respect of indisputable circumstances is unnecessary. In this regard, the court ascertains whether said persons understand correctly the contents of such circumstances, whether there are no doubts regarding voluntary nature of their position, as well as explains to them that in such a case they will be deprived of the right to challenge these circumstances by way of appeal.
