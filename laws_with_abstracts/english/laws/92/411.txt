Executive authorities and local government, within their competence, shall prepare, approve and implement the relevant road safety programmes. They are developed based on the rational combination of territorial and sectoral planning, creating interconnected balanced indicators, taking into account socio-economic and environmental conditions of a particular region, economic losses due to the death or injury (trauma) of people resulting from traffic accidents, the level of road network development, traffic accident rate and other indicators of the unified system of state road record.
