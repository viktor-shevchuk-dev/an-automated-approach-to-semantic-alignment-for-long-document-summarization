7. A person whose appeal is under consideration or his/her legal representative shall have the right to legal aid. At the person’s choice, an attorney may participate in consideration of the appeal. An attorney for providing legal aid to a person shall be appointed by the person, his/her legal representatives and other persons at the request or with the consent of the person whose appeal is under consideration. A central executive authority implementing the state policy on refugees and persons in need of subsidiary or temporary protection may appoint an attorney following the procedure determined by law through a law firm, and on the territory of administrative and territorial units where the bodies (institutions) authorised by law to provide free legal aid have commenced their activity – through the specified bodies (institutions) under the law regulating the provision of free legal aid.
